## The Math Behind a Good Opening Word

Your first guess should do one job: **eliminate as many letters as possible**. You're not trying to guess the answer on turn one — you're gathering information. The best openers are packed with common letters and, ideally, no repeats.

Strong opening words share these traits:

- **Common vowels** — most answers contain at least one of A, E, O
- **High-frequency consonants** — R, S, T, L, N do a lot of work
- **No repeated letters** on your first guess, so every slot tests something new

Classic high-coverage openers include words like CRANE, SLATE, TRACE, and AUDIO. Many players run a fixed two-word combo (one heavy on vowels, one heavy on consonants) to clear 8–10 distinct letters before they even start narrowing down.

## A Simple Method for Hard Days

1. **Open with a high-coverage word** and read the colors carefully — green (right spot), yellow (wrong spot), gray (not in the word).
2. **Second guess: spend new letters.** Don't reuse a confirmed green yet; test five fresh letters to maximize information.
3. **Now narrow.** From guess three, start placing your greens and yellows into plausible real words.
4. **Watch for repeated letters.** A huge number of failed streaks come from forgetting the answer can use the same letter twice (think ABBEY, EERIE).
5. **Mind common endings.** -ER, -LY, -ED, -OUND and similar patterns drastically shrink the candidate list.
