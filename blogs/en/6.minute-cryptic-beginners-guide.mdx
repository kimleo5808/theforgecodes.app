## What Is Minute Cryptic?

[Minute Cryptic](https://minutecryptic.org/) is a free daily puzzle that takes the hardest part of a traditional cryptic crossword — the clever clue writing — and serves up just **one clue per day**. No 15x15 grid, no 30-minute commitment. A new puzzle publishes at midnight UTC, and there's an archive of 500+ past puzzles if you want to practice.

Scoring is **golf-style: lower is better**. Your score equals the number of hints you used, so a clean solve with zero hints is the goal. It tracks your win rate and average score over time.

## The One Trick Behind Every Cryptic Clue

Here's the secret that makes the whole thing solvable: **every cryptic clue has two parts** — a *straight definition* and some *wordplay* — and they both point to the same answer.

The definition sits at the **start or end** of the clue (never the middle). The wordplay is everything else, and it builds the answer letter by letter through a trick. If your answer satisfies both halves, you're almost certainly right. That double-check is exactly why cryptics feel so satisfying when they click.

## The Six Clue Types

Almost every cryptic clue uses one of six techniques. Learn to spot the signal words and you're 80% of the way there.

| Clue type | What it does | Signal words |
|-----------|--------------|--------------|
| **Anagram** | Rearrange the letters of a word in the clue | "mixed," "broken," "confused," "out" |
| **Hidden word** | The answer is hidden inside consecutive words | "in," "part of," "some" |
| **Reversal** | Spell a word backwards | "back," "returns," "up" (in down clues) |
| **Double definition** | Two definitions of the same word, side by side | (no wordplay words — just two meanings) |
| **Charade** | Build the answer from smaller pieces joined together | "and," "with," "after" |
| **Container** | Put one set of letters inside another | "around," "inside," "holds," "in" |

## How to Solve Your First Clue

A simple, repeatable method:

1. **Find the definition.** Read the first word and the last word. One of them is a plain dictionary-style definition of the answer. This alone narrows the field hugely.
2. **Spot the signal word.** Words like "mixed" or "back" or "inside" tell you which of the six types you're dealing with.
3. **Work the wordplay.** Apply the technique — rearrange the letters, read backwards, find the hidden run — to build candidate letters.
4. **Confirm with the definition.** If the word the wordplay produces also matches the definition, you've solved it. Both halves agreeing is your proof.

> Example pattern: *"Mixed berry sounds bad (5)"* — "Mixed" signals an anagram, "berry" is the fodder... and the definition is at the end. Anagram of BERRY = "sounds bad"? You start testing arrangements. (Try it on the site — the daily clues are far better written than this.)
