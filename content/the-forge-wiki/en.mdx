## What is The Forge

The Forge is a Roblox experience inspired by the classic browser game JackSmith. Developed as a blacksmithing RPG, it drops players into a fantasy world where the core gameplay loop revolves around five interconnected systems: Mining, Forging, Combat, Exploration, and Trading. Every session follows a natural rhythm — you head into a cave to mine ores, return to your anvil to forge weapons through a timing-based minigame, equip your best gear, and venture out to fight enemies across increasingly dangerous regions.

The game stands out from typical Roblox experiences because of its depth. Rather than offering a single mechanic stretched thin, The Forge layers multiple progression systems on top of each other. Your character has a race that affects base stats, a pickaxe that determines mining efficiency, weapons and armor crafted from specific ore combinations, runes that modify gear with special abilities, and quests that unlock new areas and tools. Each system feeds into the others — better pickaxes yield rarer ores, rarer ores produce stronger weapons, stronger weapons let you clear harder regions, and harder regions contain the materials needed for the next tier of equipment.

Trading adds a social dimension to the grind. Players can exchange ores, weapons, and other items with each other, which means the in-game economy is player-driven. Rare ores and high-quality forged weapons carry real trade value, and knowing what is worth keeping versus what to sell is a skill in itself. The Forge also features a race reroll system, promotional codes for free rewards, and regular content updates that introduce new regions, enemies, and gear. Whether you are a casual player looking to forge your first sword or a veteran min-maxing your rune loadout, there is always something to work toward.

## Game World and Regions

The Forge is divided into four main regions, each with its own set of ores, enemies, NPCs, shops, and environmental hazards. Progression through the game is largely defined by which region you can survive in, and each area represents a significant jump in difficulty and reward quality.

### Stonewake's Cross

This is the starting area where every new player begins. Stonewake's Cross features basic ores like Copper, Tin, and Iron, which are used to craft entry-level weapons and armor. The enemies here are primarily zombies — slow-moving, low-HP targets that serve as an introduction to the combat system. The region contains the first set of NPCs who offer introductory quests, a basic shop for purchasing starter gear, and the anvil where you learn the forging minigame. Stonewake's Cross is designed to teach you the fundamentals without overwhelming you. Most players spend their first hour or two here before they have the gear quality to push into the next zone.

### Forgotten Kingdom

The mid-game region introduces a sharp increase in enemy variety and difficulty. You will encounter skeletons, bombers that deal area-of-effect damage, slimes with unpredictable movement patterns, reapers that hit hard and fast, and pyromancers that attack from range with fire projectiles. The ores available here — including Steel, Gold, and Platinum — produce significantly better weapons than anything from Stonewake's Cross. The Forgotten Kingdom also introduces more complex quest chains and NPC interactions. Players who can consistently clear enemies here are ready to start thinking about rune builds and optimized gear loadouts.

### Frostspire Expanse

The late-game region is where The Forge starts demanding serious preparation. Frostspire Expanse is home to spiders, orcs, yeti, and the crystal golem — enemies with high HP pools, dangerous attack patterns, and valuable loot drops. The ores found here, including Frostite and Crystal variants, are required for crafting top-tier weapons. The environment itself is harsher, with ice-themed terrain that adds visual and mechanical variety. Players entering Frostspire Expanse for the first time should have a full set of Forgotten Kingdom-tier gear at minimum, along with runes that complement their combat style.

### Volcanic Depths

The end-game region and the current pinnacle of The Forge's content. Volcanic Depths contains the rarest ores in the game, the toughest enemies, and the materials needed for the absolute best equipment. This is where players farm for Mythical-tier ores like Demonite, Darkryte, and Arcane Crystal. The enemies here punish mistakes severely, and success requires not just good gear but solid knowledge of attack patterns and positioning. Volcanic Depths is the ultimate test of everything you have learned and built throughout your playthrough.

## Races

The Forge features a 16-race system that directly affects your character's base stats and playstyle. When you first join the game, you are assigned a random race based on a weighted probability table. Races range from Common to Mythical rarity, and the rarer the race, the stronger its stat bonuses tend to be.

The full rarity breakdown is as follows: Common races like Human have a 25% drop rate and provide modest stat boosts. Uncommon races sit around 10-15% and offer slightly better bonuses. Rare races drop at roughly 3-5% and start to meaningfully impact gameplay. Epic races are in the 1-2% range with strong stat profiles. Legendary races drop below 1% and provide significant advantages. At the very top, Mythical races like Archangel have a 0.1% drop rate and come with the most powerful stat combinations in the game.

Your race is not permanent. The Wizard NPC in-game offers race rerolls for a fee, and the in-game Shop also sells reroll items. Choosing the right race depends on your playstyle. Some races favor mining efficiency, others boost combat damage or survivability, and a few provide hybrid bonuses that work well across all activities. ## Ores and Mining

Ores are the foundational resource in The Forge. Every weapon, every piece of armor, and most tradeable items originate from ores mined in the game's cave systems. Each of the four regions has its own set of ores, and within each region, ores vary by rarity — from common materials you will find on every mining run to ultra-rare drops that might take dozens of sessions to obtain.

The ore system is structured in tiers that correspond to the regions. Stonewake's Cross offers Copper, Tin, and Iron. The Forgotten Kingdom provides Steel, Gold, and Platinum. Frostspire Expanse introduces Frostite and Crystal-based ores. Volcanic Depths contains the end-game materials including Obsidian and Magma ores. Beyond the regional ores, there are special ore categories that span the entire game. The Heart ore series consists of five distinct types that can appear in any region under specific conditions. Mythical ores — Demonite, Darkryte, and Arcane Crystal — are the rarest drops in the game and are required for crafting the most powerful weapons.

Mining efficiency is not just about swinging your pickaxe. Your pickaxe's Mine Power stat determines which ores you can break, Mining Speed affects how quickly you extract them, and the Luck Boost stat increases your chance of finding rare drops. On top of that, the Luck Totem buff (available through the in-game shop or free codes) further increases rare ore spawn rates for 30 minutes. Stacking a high-Luck pickaxe with an active Luck Totem is the standard strategy for farming Mythical ores.

## Pickaxes

Pickaxes are your primary mining tool and one of the most important progression markers in The Forge. Every player starts with a free Stone Pickaxe — a basic tool with low Mine Power, slow Mining Speed, no Luck Boost, and zero Rune Slots. From there, the upgrade path spans over a dozen pickaxes, each with progressively better stats.

The four core stats on every pickaxe are Mine Power (determines which ore nodes you can break), Mining Speed (how fast you extract ore), Luck Boost (percentage increase to rare ore drops), and Rune Slots (how many runes you can attach to the pickaxe). Early pickaxes like the Iron Pickaxe and Steel Pickaxe are purchased from region shops and offer incremental improvements. Mid-game pickaxes like the Gold Pickaxe and Platinum Pickaxe start introducing Rune Slots, which opens up build customization. Late-game pickaxes such as the Crystal Pickaxe and Obsidian Pickaxe have high stats across the board and multiple Rune Slots.

At the top of the progression sit the Prismatic Pickaxe and the Dragon Head Pickaxe — end-game tools with the highest stats available. These require rare materials and significant investment to obtain. Beyond the standard upgrade path, The Forge hides several secret pickaxes in the game world. The Raven Cave contains a hidden pickaxe that requires solving an environmental puzzle to access. The Hidden Maze, a secret area within Frostspire Expanse, contains another. Finding these hidden tools is part of the exploration appeal and rewards players who look beyond the obvious paths.

## Weapons and Forging

The Forge features over 25 distinct weapon types, each with unique damage profiles, attack speeds, and special effects. Weapons are the primary output of the forging system — the core mechanic that gives the game its name. Forging takes place at the anvil and uses a timing-based minigame where you must hit specific marks on a moving bar to shape your weapon. The accuracy of your timing directly affects the quality of the finished product, which in turn determines its damage, durability, and any bonus effects.

Different ore combinations produce different weapon types. A basic sword might require Iron and Copper, while an advanced greatsword could demand Obsidian and Arcane Crystal. The recipe system encourages experimentation — trying new ore combinations is how you discover new weapon blueprints. Weapon categories include swords, greatswords, daggers, axes, hammers, spears, bows, staffs, and several special types unlocked through quests or rare ore combinations.

Weapon quality is graded on a scale that reflects your forging accuracy. A perfect timing sequence produces a weapon at maximum quality with full damage stats and the best chance of rolling bonus effects. A sloppy forge still produces a usable weapon, but with reduced stats. This means that player skill at the minigame directly translates to combat effectiveness — you cannot simply buy your way to the best gear without also mastering the forge.

Each weapon type has a distinct combat feel. Swords offer balanced speed and damage. Greatswords hit harder but swing slower. Daggers are fast but require getting close. Bows allow ranged attacks but consume ammunition. Staffs deal elemental damage and scale with specific rune builds. Choosing the right weapon for your playstyle and the enemies you are facing is a key part of The Forge's strategic depth.

## Enemies and Combat

Combat in The Forge is action-based and region-specific. Each of the four regions has its own roster of enemies, and understanding their attack patterns, HP values, damage output, and loot tables is essential for efficient progression.

In Stonewake's Cross, you face zombies — the most basic enemy type. They have low HP (around 50-100), deal minimal damage, and drop common ores and basic crafting materials. They serve as target practice for learning the combat system's dodge and attack timing. The Forgotten Kingdom escalates significantly with five enemy types: skeletons (moderate HP, melee attacks), bombers (low HP but deal heavy area damage on death), slimes (erratic movement, moderate HP), reapers (high damage, fast attacks), and pyromancers (ranged fire attacks, moderate HP). Each requires a different approach — you cannot simply tank through bombers the way you can with zombies.

Frostspire Expanse introduces enemies that demand respect. Spiders are fast and attack in groups. Orcs are tanky melee fighters with high HP pools. The yeti is a mini-boss-tier enemy with devastating charge attacks. The crystal golem is the region's apex threat — a slow but incredibly durable enemy that deals massive damage and drops some of the best loot in the game. Players farming Frostspire Expanse typically focus on spiders and orcs for consistent returns, saving golem fights for when they have optimal gear and rune setups.

Volcanic Depths enemies represent the current ceiling of combat difficulty. Every enemy here has high HP, high damage, and aggressive AI. Boss encounters in this region require specific strategies, proper gear, and often cooperation with other players. The loot rewards match the difficulty — Volcanic Depths enemies drop the rarest ores and crafting materials in the game.

## Runes

Runes are The Forge's gear enhancement system. They are items that can be socketed into equipment with available Rune Slots, adding special abilities and stat bonuses that go beyond what the base gear provides. The rune system is where build diversity really opens up — two players with identical weapons can have completely different combat profiles depending on their rune loadouts.

Rune types are categorized by the equipment slot they modify. There are runes for pickaxes (mining bonuses), runes for weapons (combat bonuses), and runes for armor (defensive bonuses). Within each category, individual runes provide specific effects: increased damage against certain enemy types, faster mining speed for specific ores, damage reduction from elemental attacks, life steal on hit, critical hit chance, and many more.

Runes are obtained through enemy drops, quest rewards, and trading with other players. Rarer runes provide stronger effects and are highly sought after in the trading economy. Building an effective rune loadout requires understanding which bonuses synergize with your race, weapon type, and the content you are farming. A player focused on mining Mythical ores in Volcanic Depths will want a very different rune setup than someone farming enemies in Frostspire Expanse for combat loot.

## Quests

The Forge includes a quest system with 11 or more named quests given by NPCs scattered across the game world. Quests provide structured objectives that reward XP, currency, unique items, and in some cases access to secret areas and tools that cannot be obtained any other way.

Quest design in The Forge ranges from simple fetch-and-deliver tasks to multi-step chains that span multiple regions. Early quests in Stonewake's Cross teach basic mechanics — mine a certain number of ores, forge your first weapon, defeat a set number of zombies. Later quests become more involved, requiring players to find hidden NPCs, solve environmental puzzles, or defeat specific enemies under certain conditions.

The rewards for completing quests are often worth more than the time investment suggests. Several quests unlock hidden pickaxes, unique weapon blueprints, and access to secret mining areas with boosted rare ore spawn rates. Quest completion also grants XP that contributes to your overall player level, which unlocks additional features and stat bonuses. For players who want to progress efficiently, following the quest chain in order is one of the fastest ways to gear up.

## Totems

Totems are purchasable buff items that provide temporary stat boosts lasting 30 minutes per activation. The Forge offers five totem types, each targeting a different aspect of gameplay:

- **Damage Totem** — Increases all damage dealt to enemies. Priced at 49 Robux.
- **XP Totem** — Boosts experience point gains from all sources. Priced at 49 Robux.
- **Luck Totem** — Increases the chance of finding rare ores while mining. Priced at 75 Robux.
- **Health Totem** — Increases maximum HP and adds passive health regeneration. Priced at 49 Robux.
- **Mining Totem** — Boosts mining speed and ore yield. Priced at 49 Robux.

The Luck Totem is the most popular choice among experienced players because rare ore drops are the primary bottleneck in late-game progression. Stacking a Luck Totem with a high-Luck pickaxe and a race that provides mining bonuses creates the optimal setup for farming Mythical ores.

While totems are primarily a Robux purchase, free totems are regularly distributed through promotional codes.
