## All Ores in The Forge — Complete Mining Guide (2026)

Ores are the foundation of everything you do in [The Forge](/the-forge-wiki). Every weapon you swing, every piece of armor you wear, and every upgrade you chase starts with a pickaxe hitting rock inside a cave. The Forge is a blacksmithing RPG on Roblox where the core gameplay loop revolves around mining ores, smelting them at the furnace, and forging them into gear at the anvil. Without ores, there is no progression.

The game currently features over 30 distinct ores spread across four regions: Stonewake's Cross, Forgotten Kingdom, Frostspire Expanse, and Volcanic Depths. Each region introduces progressively rarer and more valuable ores, and each ore has a specific rarity tier that determines how often it spawns, how much it sells for, and what it can craft. Rarity tiers range from Common all the way up to Mythical, and the jump in value between tiers is significant.

This guide covers every ore in the game organized by region, including rarity, approximate sell value, and primary crafting uses. Whether you are a new player trying to figure out what to mine first or a veteran hunting for Mythical drops, this page has you covered. We also include tips on mining efficiency, pickaxe recommendations, and links to related guides like the [pickaxes guide](/the-forge-pickaxes-guide) and [weapons guide](/the-forge-weapons-guide) so you can plan your entire progression path from one place.

## How Mining Works in The Forge

Mining in The Forge is straightforward in concept but has enough depth to reward players who understand the system. To mine, you enter a cave in any of the four regions. Caves are accessible from the overworld — walk up to a cave entrance and interact with it. Once inside, you will see ore veins embedded in the cave walls. Walk up to a vein and use your pickaxe to start mining. Each swing chips away at the vein until the ore drops into your inventory.

Your mining speed depends on your [pickaxe](/the-forge-pickaxes-guide) stats. Better pickaxes mine faster and have higher base luck, which directly affects the quality of ores you pull from each vein. The pickaxe you carry is the single most important factor in your mining efficiency, so upgrading it should always be a priority.

Beyond pickaxe stats, two other factors influence your mining results. First, your [race](/the-forge-race-tier-list) can provide passive mining bonuses. Some races increase mining speed, others boost luck, and a few grant bonus ore drops on each swing. Choosing the right race for a mining-focused build makes a noticeable difference over time. Second, [totems](/the-forge-totems-guide) provide temporary but powerful buffs. The Luck Totem is the most important one for miners — it increases the chance of pulling rare and legendary ores from any vein. Some [the forge codes](/the-forge-codes) give free Luck Totems, so always check for active codes before a mining session.

Cave depth also matters. The deeper you go into a cave, the higher the chance of encountering rare ore veins. Some ores only spawn past certain depth thresholds, and a few are hidden behind breakable walls that require a strong enough pickaxe to destroy. Pay attention to visual cues — rare veins often have a subtle glow or distinct color compared to common ones.

## Stonewake's Cross Ores

Stonewake's Cross is the starting region in The Forge and where every player begins their mining journey. The ores here are low in value and rarity, but they are essential for learning the forging system and crafting your first set of weapons. Do not skip these ores — you need them to complete early quests and unlock access to better regions.

| Ore | Rarity | Value | Crafting Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stone | Common | 5 | Basic handles, repair materials |
| Copper | Common | 10 | Copper Sword, Copper Dagger |
| Tin | Common | 10 | Tin Shield, Bronze alloy component |
| Coal | Common | 8 | Fuel for smelting, torch crafting |
| Iron | Uncommon | 25 | Iron Sword, Iron Armor set |
| Silver | Uncommon | 35 | Silver Blade, Silver accessories |

Stone and Coal are the most abundant ores in Stonewake's Cross and spawn on nearly every wall. Copper and Tin appear frequently as well and are the first ores you will use at the anvil. Iron and Silver are Uncommon, meaning they spawn less often but are still easy to find if you explore the full cave. Iron in particular is a key milestone ore — the Iron Sword is a significant upgrade over starter weapons and will carry you into the Forgotten Kingdom.

The caves in Stonewake's Cross are shallow and straightforward. There are no hidden areas or breakable walls to worry about. Focus on filling your inventory with Iron and Silver before moving on, and complete any [quests](/the-forge-quests-guide) that require Stonewake ores while you are here. Selling excess Stone and Coal to vendors is a decent way to earn early gold for pickaxe upgrades.

## Forgotten Kingdom Ores

The Forgotten Kingdom is the second region and represents a major step up in ore quality. The caves here are deeper, the veins are harder to mine, and the ores are worth significantly more. You will need at least an Iron Pickaxe to mine efficiently here — anything less and your mining speed drops noticeably.

| Ore | Rarity | Value | Crafting Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Gold | Rare | 75 | Gold Longsword, Gold Armor set |
| Platinum | Rare | 90 | Platinum Blade, Platinum Shield |
| Ruby Ore | Rare | 100 | Ruby-Encrusted Sword, jewelry |
| Obsidian | Epic | 150 | Obsidian Greatsword, Obsidian Armor |
| Mithril | Epic | 200 | Mithril Rapier, Mithril Armor set |

Gold is the most common ore in the Forgotten Kingdom and serves as the baseline resource for mid-game crafting. Platinum is slightly rarer and crafts into marginally better gear. Ruby Ore is a special case — it is classified as Rare but has a lower spawn rate than Gold or Platinum, and the weapons it crafts have unique visual effects that make them popular among players.

Obsidian and Mithril are the real prizes here. Both are Epic rarity, which means they spawn infrequently and often in deeper sections of the cave. Obsidian veins are dark and blend into shadowy cave walls, so keep your eyes open. Mithril has a distinctive blue-silver shimmer that makes it easier to spot. The Mithril Armor set is one of the best mid-game armor options and will serve you well into the Frostspire Expanse.

If you are struggling to find Epic ores, activate a Luck Totem before entering the cave. The difference in spawn rates with and without the totem is substantial. Check our [totems guide](/the-forge-totems-guide) for details on totem duration and stacking.

## Frostspire Expanse Ores

Frostspire Expanse is the third region and where the game starts to feel genuinely challenging. The caves are long, multi-layered, and filled with tougher [enemies](/the-forge-enemies-guide) that can interrupt your mining. You need a Mithril Pickaxe or better to mine here without wasting time, and bringing combat gear is strongly recommended.

| Ore | Rarity | Value | Crafting Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Crystal | Epic | 250 | Crystal Blade, Crystal Armor set |
| Sapphire Ore | Epic | 275 | Sapphire Edge, enchanted accessories |
| Diamond | Epic | 350 | Diamond Greatsword, Diamond Armor |
| Prismarine | Legendary | 500 | Prismarine Katana, Prismarine Shield |
| Frostite | Legendary | 550 | Frostite Claymore, Frostite Armor set |

Crystal is the entry-level ore for Frostspire and spawns in the upper sections of the caves. It has a translucent appearance that catches light, making it relatively easy to spot. Sapphire Ore is similar in rarity but slightly more valuable and tends to spawn in clusters rather than single veins. Diamond is the most sought-after Epic ore in the game — the Diamond Armor set provides excellent defense and the Diamond Greatsword hits hard enough to handle most enemies in the region.

Prismarine and Frostite are Legendary rarity, which is a significant jump. Legendary ores have noticeably lower spawn rates and tend to appear only in the deepest parts of Frostspire caves. Prismarine veins emit a faint rainbow shimmer, while Frostite glows with an icy blue aura. Both are used to craft some of the strongest weapons available before reaching the Volcanic Depths. The Frostite Claymore in particular is a fan favorite for its high damage output and frost effect on hit.

Mining in Frostspire without a Luck Totem active is possible but frustrating if you are targeting Legendary ores. The base spawn rate for Prismarine and Frostite is low enough that you can clear an entire cave without seeing one. With a Luck Totem active, you will typically find at least one or two Legendary veins per cave run. Plan your totem usage around your Frostspire sessions for maximum efficiency.

## Volcanic Depths Ores

Volcanic Depths is the final and most dangerous region in [The Forge](/the-forge-wiki). The caves here are filled with lava hazards, the strongest enemies in the game, and environmental damage that chips away at your health. You need a Diamond Pickaxe or better to mine here, and you should bring your best [weapons](/the-forge-weapons-guide) and armor. This is end-game content.

| Ore | Rarity | Value | Crafting Use |
|---|---|---|---|
| Magma Ore | Legendary | 750 | Magma Blade, Magma Armor set |
| Volcanic Crystal | Legendary | 850 | Volcanic Greatsword, Volcanic Shield |
| Infernal Ore | Mythical | 1500 | Infernal Edge, Infernal Armor set |

There are only three ores in the Volcanic Depths, but each one is extraordinarily valuable. Magma Ore is the most common of the three — and even it is Legendary rarity. It glows with a deep orange-red light and is found along the walls of lava-adjacent tunnels. Volcanic Crystal is rarer and spawns in crystalline formations near the deepest lava pools. It has a dark red-purple hue that distinguishes it from Magma Ore.

Infernal Ore is the crown jewel of the Volcanic Depths and one of the rarest ores in the entire game. It is Mythical rarity, meaning its spawn rate is extremely low even with a Luck Totem active. Infernal Ore veins emit a pulsing flame effect and are unmistakable when you find one. The Infernal Edge and Infernal Armor set crafted from this ore are among the most powerful items in The Forge, rivaled only by weapons forged from the three standalone Mythical ores.

Surviving long enough to mine in the Volcanic Depths requires preparation. Bring fire resistance potions if available, keep your health topped up, and clear enemies before you start mining. Getting interrupted mid-swing by a Volcanic Guardian is a common way to lose progress.

## Special Ores — Heart Series

The Heart series is a unique set of five ores that do not belong to any specific region. Instead, they can spawn in caves across all four regions, though their appearance rate is extremely low regardless of location. Heart ores are some of the most coveted resources in The Forge because of their unique crafting properties and the prestige that comes with finding one.

The five Heart ores are: **Heart of Stone**, **Heart of Iron**, **Heart of Gold**, **Heart of Crystal**, and **Heart of Fire**. Each one corresponds loosely to a region's theme — Heart of Stone appears most often in Stonewake's Cross, Heart of Iron in the Forgotten Kingdom, and so on — but any Heart ore can technically spawn anywhere. The key visual difference is that Heart ores glow with a pulsing, heartbeat-like animation that normal ores do not have. When you see a vein with a rhythmic glow, you have found a Heart ore.

Mining a Heart ore also triggers a distinct animation — your character pulls the ore free in a dramatic fashion rather than the standard chipping animation. This makes the moment feel special and is a nice touch by the developers.

In terms of crafting, Heart ores are used to create Heart-infused weapons and armor. These items have unique passive effects that standard gear does not offer. For example, Heart of Fire weapons deal bonus fire damage over time, while Heart of Crystal gear provides a chance to reflect incoming damage. Heart-infused items sit between Legendary and Mythical tier in terms of power, making them excellent options for players who have not yet obtained Mythical ores.

The drop rate for Heart ores is estimated at less than 1% per vein, even with a Luck Totem active. If you are specifically farming for Heart ores, your best strategy is to run caves repeatedly in the region that matches the Heart ore you want, with a Luck Totem active and a [race](/the-forge-race-tier-list) that has luck bonuses.

## Mythical Ores

At the very top of the ore hierarchy sit three Mythical ores: **Demonite**, **Darkryte**, and **Arcane Crystal**. These are the rarest resources in The Forge and the materials required to craft the most powerful weapons in the game. Finding even one of these ores is a milestone achievement that most players celebrate.

**Demonite** spawns exclusively in the deepest sections of Volcanic Depths caves, often behind breakable walls that require a Diamond Pickaxe or better to destroy. It has a dark purple-black appearance with swirling particle effects. Demonite is used to craft the Demonite Scythe and Demonite Armor, both of which have life-steal effects that make them incredibly powerful for sustained combat.

**Darkryte** is found in hidden cave areas across the Forgotten Kingdom and Frostspire Expanse. Unlike most ores, Darkryte veins do not appear on normal cave walls — they spawn inside secret rooms that are accessed through breakable walls or hidden passages. Darkryte has a void-like appearance, almost as if it absorbs light around it. The Darkryte Blade crafted from this ore deals shadow damage and has a chance to blind enemies on hit.

**Arcane Crystal** is arguably the rarest ore in the entire game. It can spawn in any region but has an astronomically low drop rate. Arcane Crystal veins emit a bright, multicolored glow that is impossible to miss — the challenge is simply finding one. The Arcane Staff and Arcane Armor crafted from this ore provide massive boosts to all stats and are considered the best-in-slot items for end-game players.

All three Mythical ores have estimated drop rates below 0.1% per vein. Even with a Luck Totem, finding them requires patience and persistence. Many veteran players report mining for dozens of hours before finding their first Mythical ore. If you are hunting for these, maximize every advantage — use Luck Totems, pick a [race with luck bonuses](/the-forge-race-tier-list), and focus on deep cave runs in the appropriate regions.

## Mining Efficiency Tips

Getting the most out of every cave run comes down to preparation and route optimization. Here are the most impactful things you can do to improve your mining efficiency in The Forge.

**Use the right pickaxe for each region.** Your pickaxe determines your mining speed and luck stat. Using an underpowered pickaxe in a high-level region wastes enormous amounts of time. Check our [pickaxes guide](/the-forge-pickaxes-guide) for the recommended pickaxe at each stage of the game. As a quick reference: Starter Pickaxe for Stonewake's Cross, Iron Pickaxe for Forgotten Kingdom, Mithril Pickaxe for Frostspire Expanse, and Diamond Pickaxe for Volcanic Depths.

**Activate a Luck Totem before every serious mining session.** The Luck Totem increases rare ore spawn rates across all regions. The effect is most noticeable when you are targeting Epic, Legendary, or Mythical ores. Totems have a limited duration, so plan your cave route before activating one to avoid wasting time. See the [totems guide](/the-forge-totems-guide) for full details on totem effects and duration.

**Choose a race with mining bonuses.** Several races in The Forge provide passive bonuses to mining speed or luck. If mining is your primary activity, rerolling for a mining-focused race is worth the investment. Our [race tier list](/the-forge-race-tier-list) ranks every race and highlights which ones are best for miners.

**Mine deep and mine fast.** Rare ores spawn more frequently in deeper cave sections. When you enter a cave, head straight for the back rather than mining every vein near the entrance. The common ores near the entrance are not worth your time if you are after rare drops. Clear the deep sections first, then work your way back out if you have inventory space.

**Check for active codes.** Some [the forge codes](/the-forge-codes) grant free Luck Totems, gems for pickaxe upgrades, or other mining-related rewards. Always check for new codes before starting a long mining session — a free Luck Totem can save you significant resources.

**Watch for breakable walls.** In the Forgotten Kingdom, Frostspire Expanse, and Volcanic Depths, some cave sections have breakable walls that hide secret rooms containing rare ore veins. These walls have a slightly different texture and crack pattern compared to normal walls. Hit them with your pickaxe to break through.

## Ore to Weapon Crafting Reference

Every ore in The Forge feeds into the crafting system. The general rule is simple: higher rarity ores craft higher tier weapons and armor. Here is a quick overview of how ore tiers map to weapon tiers.

**Common ores** (Stone, Copper, Tin, Coal) craft basic weapons — Copper Sword, Tin Shield, and similar starter gear. These weapons have low damage but are sufficient for Stonewake's Cross content.

**Uncommon ores** (Iron, Silver) craft improved weapons like the Iron Sword and Silver Blade. These are your first real upgrades and will carry you through early Forgotten Kingdom content.

**Rare ores** (Gold, Platinum, Ruby Ore) craft mid-tier weapons with decent stats. Gold and Platinum weapons are solid all-rounders, while Ruby weapons have unique visual effects.

**Epic ores** (Obsidian, Mithril, Crystal, Sapphire, Diamond) craft powerful weapons that define the mid-to-late game. Diamond gear in particular is a major power spike.

**Legendary ores** (Prismarine, Frostite, Magma Ore, Volcanic Crystal) craft top-tier weapons with special effects like frost damage or fire damage. These are end-game viable.

**Mythical ores** (Demonite, Darkryte, Arcane Crystal, Infernal Ore) craft the absolute best weapons in the game. Each Mythical weapon has a unique passive effect that no other weapon can replicate.

For full crafting recipes, ore requirements, and forging instructions, see our complete [weapons guide](/the-forge-weapons-guide).

## FAQ

### What is the rarest ore in The Forge?

Arcane Crystal is widely considered the rarest ore in The Forge. It is Mythical rarity, can spawn in any region, and has an estimated drop rate below 0.1% per vein. Even with a Luck Totem active and a luck-boosting [race](/the-forge-race-tier-list), finding Arcane Crystal requires significant time investment. Demonite and Darkryte are similarly rare but are at least confined to specific regions, which makes targeted farming slightly more practical.

### How do I find Mythical ores?

Mythical ores — Demonite, Darkryte, and Arcane Crystal — spawn in the deepest sections of caves and behind breakable walls. To maximize your chances, use a Luck Totem, equip the best [pickaxe](/the-forge-pickaxes-guide) available, choose a race with luck bonuses, and focus on deep cave runs. Demonite is found in Volcanic Depths, Darkryte in hidden rooms in Forgotten Kingdom and Frostspire Expanse, and Arcane Crystal can appear anywhere. Patience is essential — these ores are meant to be rare.

### Does the Luck Totem affect ore drops?

Yes. The Luck Totem directly increases the spawn rate of rare, epic, legendary, and mythical ore veins in caves. The effect applies to all regions and stacks with race-based luck bonuses. Activating a Luck Totem before entering a cave is the single most effective way to improve your chances of finding valuable ores. Check our [totems guide](/the-forge-totems-guide) for details on duration and how to obtain Luck Totems. Some [the forge codes](/the-forge-codes) also give free Luck Totems.

### Can I trade ores with other players?

The Forge does support a trading system that allows players to exchange ores and other items. To initiate a trade, walk up to another player and use the trade interaction. Both players must confirm the trade before it completes. Trading is a great way to obtain specific ores you need for crafting without having to farm them yourself. However, Mythical ores are highly valued in the trading economy, so expect to offer significant resources in return.

### What pickaxe do I need for Volcanic Depths?

You need at minimum a Diamond Pickaxe to mine effectively in the Volcanic Depths. Using anything weaker results in extremely slow mining speed and an inability to break through the reinforced walls that hide some of the best ore veins. Ideally, you want a fully upgraded Diamond Pickaxe or better. Check our [pickaxes guide](/the-forge-pickaxes-guide) for upgrade paths and where to obtain the Diamond Pickaxe.
