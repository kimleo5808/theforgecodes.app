## What is The Forge

The Forge is a blacksmithing RPG on Roblox inspired by the classic browser game JackSmith. It combines mining, crafting, combat, and trading into a single experience that rewards patience, skill, and smart decision-making. As a new player, you spawn into a fantasy world where your primary goal is to mine ores from caves, forge those ores into weapons and armor at an anvil, equip your crafted gear, and fight enemies across increasingly dangerous regions. The game features a deep progression system built around races, runes, quests, totems, and multiple interconnected upgrade paths that keep the gameplay loop engaging for dozens of hours.

What makes The Forge stand out from other Roblox experiences is the sheer number of systems layered on top of each other. Your character has a race that provides unique stat bonuses and passive abilities. Your pickaxe determines what ores you can mine and how efficiently you gather them. The weapons and armor you forge depend on the ores you collect, and the quality of your forging depends on your timing skill at the anvil. Runes modify your gear with special effects, totems provide temporary boosts during mining and combat, and quests guide you through the game world while rewarding you with gold, experience, and exclusive items.

The game world is divided into four main regions — Stonewake's Cross, Forgotten Kingdom, Frostspire Expanse, and Volcanic Depths — each with its own set of ores, enemies, NPCs, and challenges. Progression through these regions is gated by your gear quality and combat ability, so every upgrade you make matters. Trading with other players adds a social economy layer where rare ores and high-quality weapons carry real value. Whether you want to focus on mining, combat, or a balanced approach, The Forge has a path for you.

This guide walks you through everything you need to know as a brand new player. We cover your first five minutes, the core gameplay loop, mining and forging basics, combat essentials, the ten most common beginner mistakes, an optimal 30-minute plan, progression paths, and frequently asked questions. For a complete reference on every game system, visit the [The Forge Wiki](/the-forge-wiki).

## Your First 5 Minutes

When you first join The Forge, you spawn in Stonewake's Cross — the starting region. The game immediately assigns you a random race. Do not worry about your race right now. Every race is viable for early-game content, and you can reroll your race later using free rerolls from [active codes](/the-forge-codes). Your starting race will not hold you back in any meaningful way during your first several hours of play.

You also receive a free Stone Pickaxe in your inventory. This is the most basic mining tool in the game, but it is all you need to get started. The Stone Pickaxe can mine Stone and Copper ores, which are the two most common materials in Stonewake's Cross caves. It mines slowly compared to upgraded pickaxes, but speed is not your concern right now — learning the mechanics is.

Your first action should be talking to the tutorial NPC standing near the spawn point. This NPC walks you through the basic controls: how to mine, how to forge, how to equip gear, and how to fight. Skipping this tutorial is one of the most common beginner mistakes because the NPC also gives you a small gold reward for completing the dialogue. Take the two minutes to finish it.

After the tutorial, head toward the nearest cave entrance in Stonewake's Cross. You will see a glowing opening in the rock face — walk up to it and interact to enter. Inside the cave, look for ore veins on the walls. They appear as colored patches of rock. Walk up to a vein and use your pickaxe to start mining. Collect a mix of Stone and Copper ores — aim for at least 5-8 pieces total. Once your inventory has enough material, exit the cave and head to the anvil in the center of Stonewake's Cross.

At the anvil, you will enter the forging minigame for the first time. Select a weapon blueprint — the Copper Sword is a solid first choice — and follow the timing prompts to hammer the weapon into shape. Do not stress about getting a perfect score on your first attempt. Even a Common quality weapon is strong enough to handle the early enemies. Equip your newly forged weapon and you are officially ready to fight. Head back into the caves and look for zombies to test your combat skills. For a full breakdown of every ore and where to find them, check the [ores guide](/the-forge-ores-guide).

## Understanding the Core Loop

The Forge is built around a simple but deeply satisfying gameplay loop: Mine, Forge, Fight, Upgrade, Repeat. Every session follows this rhythm, and understanding it early will help you make efficient decisions about where to spend your time and resources.

The loop starts with mining. You enter a cave, find ore veins, and use your pickaxe to collect raw materials. The type and rarity of ores you can mine depends on your pickaxe tier and the region you are in. Early on, you are limited to Stone and Copper in Stonewake's Cross. As you upgrade your pickaxe and unlock new regions, you gain access to Iron, Steel, Gold, Platinum, and eventually rare and mythical ores in the late-game areas.

Next comes forging. You take your mined ores to the anvil and craft them into weapons and armor through a timing-based minigame. The quality of your forged item depends on how accurately you hit the timing markers. Higher quality gear has better stats, which directly impacts your combat effectiveness. The forging system rewards practice — the more you forge, the better your timing becomes, and the higher quality items you produce consistently.

Then you fight. Equip your best gear and head into caves or overworld areas to battle enemies. Combat earns you gold and experience points. Gold is used to buy pickaxe upgrades, consumables, and other items from NPC shops. Experience points level up your character, unlocking access to new regions and content. Defeating tougher enemies yields better rewards, which feeds directly back into the upgrade phase.

The upgrade phase is where you spend your earned gold and resources. Buy a better pickaxe from Miner Fred to mine faster and access higher-tier ores. Forge better weapons with those new ores. Apply [runes](/the-forge-runes-guide) to your gear for special effects. Use [totems](/the-forge-totems-guide) for temporary boosts during important runs. Complete [quests](/the-forge-quests-guide) for unique rewards. Every upgrade makes the next cycle more productive, creating a satisfying progression spiral that keeps pulling you forward.

The key insight for beginners is that every part of the loop feeds into every other part. Skipping any step creates a bottleneck. If you only mine but never forge, you are sitting on unused resources. If you only fight but never upgrade your pickaxe, you are stuck with low-tier ores. Balance your time across all four phases and you will progress smoothly. The [wiki](/the-forge-wiki) has detailed breakdowns of each system if you want to dive deeper into any specific mechanic.

## Mining Basics

Mining is the foundation of all progression in The Forge. Without ores, you cannot forge weapons, and without weapons, you cannot fight enemies or progress to new regions. Understanding how mining works and how to do it efficiently will save you hours of grinding.

To mine, enter any cave in the game world. Caves are found in every region and are marked by glowing entrances on the overworld map. Once inside, you will see ore veins embedded in the cave walls — they look like colored patches of rock that stand out from the surrounding stone. Walk up to a vein and use your equipped pickaxe to start mining. Each swing chips away at the vein until the ore drops into your inventory. Different ores take different numbers of swings to extract, and your pickaxe tier affects both mining speed and which ores you can actually mine.

Your starting Stone Pickaxe can only mine Stone and Copper ores. To mine Iron, you need at least a Copper Pickaxe. To mine Steel and Gold, you need an Iron Pickaxe or better. This tiered system means that upgrading your pickaxe is one of the most important investments you can make. Each pickaxe upgrade opens up new ores, which in turn allow you to forge stronger weapons and armor. Check the [pickaxes guide](/the-forge-pickaxes-guide) for the full upgrade path and costs.

Luck is a hidden stat that affects your chance of finding rare ore spawns. Some races have natural luck bonuses, and certain [runes](/the-forge-runes-guide) and [totems](/the-forge-totems-guide) can temporarily boost your luck. When you are mining for rare ores in late-game regions, luck becomes a critical stat. For early game, though, focus on upgrading your pickaxe tier rather than chasing luck bonuses.

A practical tip for new miners: do not leave a cave until your inventory is full or nearly full. Each cave entrance has a loading screen, so minimizing trips in and out saves time. Also, pay attention to which ores spawn in which caves — not all caves in a region contain the same ore distribution. Some caves have higher concentrations of specific ores, and learning these patterns will make your mining runs more efficient. The [ores guide](/the-forge-ores-guide) lists every ore by region and rarity so you know exactly what to look for.

## Forging Your First Weapons

Forging is where your mined ores become usable gear. The forging system in The Forge is a timing-based minigame that takes place at the anvil. Every region has at least one anvil, and the one in Stonewake's Cross is located in the central plaza near the spawn point.

To start forging, interact with the anvil and select a weapon or armor blueprint from the menu. Each blueprint requires specific ores and a minimum forging level. Early blueprints like the Copper Sword and Copper Shield only require Copper ore and are available from the start. As you forge more items, your forging experience increases, unlocking access to more advanced blueprints that use rarer ores and produce stronger gear.

The minigame itself works like a rhythm game. A marker moves along a bar, and you need to press the action button when the marker is inside the target zone. The closer your timing is to the center of the target zone, the higher the quality of the finished item. Quality tiers range from Common at the bottom to Legendary at the top. Higher quality items have better base stats — a Legendary Copper Sword deals significantly more damage than a Common one, even though they use the same materials.

Do not stress about hitting perfect timing on every forge during your first few hours. Common and Uncommon quality weapons are perfectly adequate for Stonewake's Cross content. The zombies and early enemies in this region have low HP and deal minimal damage, so even a basic weapon will get the job done. Use your early forging sessions to practice the rhythm and get comfortable with the timing window. As you progress to harder regions, forging quality becomes more important, and by then you will have had plenty of practice.

One important detail: forging consumes your ores permanently. If you forge a weapon and it comes out Common quality, those ores are gone. You cannot disassemble a forged item back into raw materials. This means you should avoid forging with rare ores until you are confident in your timing ability. Practice with cheap Stone and Copper ores first. When you are consistently hitting Rare or Epic quality, then start using your valuable materials. For a complete list of all weapon blueprints and their ore requirements, see the [weapons guide](/the-forge-weapons-guide).

## Combat Essentials

Combat in The Forge is real-time and action-based. You control your character directly, using left-click to attack and right-click to block. The combat system is simple to learn but has enough depth to reward skilled play, especially in later regions where enemies hit hard and have varied attack patterns.

Your primary attack is a left-click swing with your equipped weapon. Different weapon types have different attack speeds, ranges, and damage values. Swords are the most balanced option — moderate speed, moderate range, moderate damage. Daggers are fast but short-range, requiring you to stay close to enemies. Hammers are slow but deal heavy damage and can stagger enemies. Spears have the longest reach but narrow hit zones. For beginners, swords are the safest choice because they do not punish positioning mistakes as harshly as other weapon types.

Blocking with right-click reduces incoming damage significantly. Holding block before an enemy attack lands will absorb most of the hit. However, the real power move is the perfect parry. If you time your block to activate at the exact moment an enemy attack connects, you perform a perfect parry that completely negates the damage and stuns the enemy for a short window. Stunned enemies cannot attack or move, giving you free hits. Mastering the parry timing is one of the most important combat skills in the game, and it becomes essential in the Forgotten Kingdom and beyond where enemies deal serious damage.

Every enemy in the game has a level indicator displayed above their head. As a general rule, do not fight enemies more than two levels above your current gear tier. A level 5 zombie in Stonewake's Cross is manageable with Copper gear, but a level 15 skeleton in the Forgotten Kingdom will destroy you if you are still wearing Iron armor. Check the [enemies guide](/the-forge-enemies-guide) for a full breakdown of every enemy type, their attack patterns, and recommended gear levels for each encounter.

Combat also rewards you with gold and experience points on each kill. Gold drops scale with enemy difficulty — harder enemies drop more gold. Experience points contribute to your overall character level, which gates access to new regions and unlocks certain NPC interactions. Farming enemies efficiently is a core part of the progression loop, so finding the right balance between enemy difficulty and kill speed is key. Fight the hardest enemies you can kill quickly and consistently, rather than struggling against enemies that take too long to defeat.

3. **Fighting enemies that are too high level.** Each enemy has a level indicator. If an enemy is more than two tiers above your gear, you will deal minimal damage and take massive hits. Stick to enemies appropriate for your current equipment and work your way up gradually.

4. **Not using the block and parry mechanic.** Many beginners just spam left-click and ignore blocking entirely. This works in Stonewake's Cross where enemies are weak, but it will get you killed in every other region. Practice blocking and parrying early so it becomes second nature.

7. **Ignoring quests.** Quests from NPCs provide gold, experience, and sometimes exclusive items that you cannot get any other way. Many new players skip quest dialogue and miss out on these rewards. Talk to every NPC with a quest marker and complete their tasks. See the [quests guide](/the-forge-quests-guide) for a full quest list.

8. **Not using totems during important mining runs.** Totems provide temporary boosts to luck, mining speed, and other stats. 9. **Skipping the tutorial NPC.** The tutorial NPC gives you a gold reward and teaches you mechanics that are not obvious from gameplay alone. It takes two minutes and is always worth doing.

10. **Trying to rush to late-game regions without proper gear.** Frostspire Expanse and Volcanic Depths will punish undergeared players severely. You need at minimum Steel-tier weapons and armor to survive in Frostspire, and Gold-tier or above for Volcanic Depths. Trying to skip ahead just means dying repeatedly and wasting time. ## Progression Paths

Once you have the basics down, The Forge opens up into three main playstyles. You do not have to commit to one exclusively — most players blend elements of all three — but understanding these paths helps you prioritize upgrades and choose the right race.

### Mining Focus

The mining-focused path prioritizes pickaxe upgrades, luck bonuses, and Fortune runes above all else. Your goal is to become the most efficient miner possible, finding rare ores consistently and using them to craft top-tier weapons for yourself or to trade with other players. This path turns you into a resource generator — you may not be the strongest fighter, but you will have access to the best crafting materials in the game.

Best races for mining focus are Dwarf and Elf. Dwarf provides natural mining speed bonuses and increased ore yield, making every mining run more productive. Elf offers luck bonuses that increase rare ore spawn rates, which becomes incredibly valuable in late-game regions where Mythical ores are the ultimate prize. For runes, prioritize Fortune (increased rare drops) and Haste (faster mining speed). Use luck totems before every mining run in Frostspire Expanse and Volcanic Depths where the rare ores are worth the most. Check the [race tier list](/the-forge-race-tier-list) for detailed stat comparisons.

### Combat Focus

The combat-focused path prioritizes weapon upgrades, damage runes, and aggressive stat builds. Your goal is to defeat the toughest enemies and bosses in the game, farming rare combat drops and pushing into the hardest content as quickly as possible. Combat-focused players often trade for ores rather than mining them, using their gold from enemy drops to buy materials from mining-focused players.

Best races for combat focus are Orc, Demon, and Dragonborn. Orc provides raw damage bonuses and increased HP, making you a straightforward powerhouse. Demon offers lifesteal and critical hit bonuses that reward aggressive play. Dragonborn has fire resistance and area-of-effect damage abilities that excel against groups of enemies. For runes, prioritize Fury (increased damage), Vampiric (lifesteal on hit), and Fortify (damage reduction). The [runes guide](/the-forge-runes-guide) has full details on every rune and optimal combat builds.

### Balanced Path

The balanced path upgrades mining and combat capabilities evenly. This is the recommended approach for new players because it avoids the bottlenecks that come from over-specializing too early. You mine your own ores, forge your own weapons, and fight your own battles without relying on trading to fill gaps in your progression.

Most races work well for a balanced approach. Human is a solid default choice with no weaknesses and small bonuses across all stats. Angel and Vampire are also strong balanced options with useful passive abilities. The balanced path takes slightly longer to reach peak performance in any single area, but it gives you the most self-sufficient and flexible gameplay experience. You can always specialize later once you understand which aspect of the game you enjoy most.
