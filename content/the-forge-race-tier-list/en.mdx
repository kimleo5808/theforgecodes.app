# The Forge Race Tier List — All 16 Races Ranked (2026)

Races are one of the most important systems in The Forge. When you first join the game, you are randomly assigned a race that determines your base stats, passive abilities, and overall playstyle. Some races give you massive combat damage bonuses, others boost your mining luck or health regeneration, and a few offer almost nothing at all. The race you roll can genuinely shape how you experience the game from your very first session.

There are 16 races in total, spread across five rarity tiers from Common all the way up to Mythical. The rarer the race, the stronger its passive bonuses tend to be. Getting a top-tier race through a random roll is extremely unlikely — Archangel, the rarest race in the game, has just a 0.1% drop rate. That means on average you would need around 1,000 rolls to land it once.

The good news is that you are not stuck with whatever you get on your first try. You can reroll your race using the Wizard NPC or through the in-game Shop, and the easiest way to get free rerolls is by redeeming [the forge codes](/the-forge-codes). Most active codes give rerolls as part of their reward, so checking for new codes regularly is one of the smartest things you can do as a player.

This guide ranks every race in the game, breaks down their individual strengths, and gives you a clear reroll strategy so you know exactly when to keep what you have and when to try again. Whether you are a new player trying to understand what you rolled or a veteran deciding if it is worth burning rerolls, this tier list has you covered.

## S-Tier Races — The Rarest and Most Powerful

The three S-tier races are the ones every player hopes to land. They have the lowest drop rates in the game and offer the strongest stat bonuses by a wide margin. If you roll any of these, do not reroll under any circumstances.

### Archangel (0.1% Drop Rate)

Archangel is the single rarest race in The Forge and arguably the most powerful thing you can have on your character. At a 0.1% chance per roll, most players will never see it naturally. Archangel provides a significant boost to combat damage, health regeneration, and mining luck all at once. It is the only race in the game that excels in every category simultaneously. The health regen alone makes you nearly unkillable in early and mid-game content, while the damage bonus keeps you competitive in late-game combat zones. Players who roll Archangel often find that the entire game feels easier — mobs die faster, ores drop more frequently, and survivability is never an issue. If you somehow land this race, consider yourself extremely lucky and never touch the reroll button again.

### Angel (0.5% Drop Rate)

Angel is the second rarest race and focuses heavily on healing and defensive stats. Angel players receive a strong health regeneration passive that ticks constantly, a moderate boost to maximum health, and a small but noticeable increase to combat damage. Where Angel really shines is in sustained fights — while other races might need to retreat and heal, Angel players can keep pushing through damage because their regen outpaces most incoming hits in early and mid-game areas. Angel also provides a minor mining luck bonus, making it a well-rounded choice even outside of combat. It is not quite as dominant as Archangel across the board, but it is still firmly in the top tier and absolutely worth keeping if you roll it.

### Demon (0.5% Drop Rate)

Demon is the offensive counterpart to Angel. Where Angel focuses on staying alive, Demon focuses on killing things as fast as possible. Demon players get the highest raw combat damage bonus of any race in the game, along with a small lifesteal effect that heals you for a percentage of damage dealt. This combination makes Demon incredibly effective in combat zones — you hit hard and heal just enough to stay in the fight without needing to disengage. The trade-off is that Demon offers almost no mining bonuses, so if your primary focus is ore farming, Angel or Archangel would serve you better. But for players who spend most of their time fighting mobs and pushing into harder zones, Demon is the ideal race. Like Angel, it sits at a 0.5% drop rate, so rolling it is a genuine achievement.

## A-Tier Races — Strong and Worth Keeping

A-tier races are rare enough that most players will not see them in their first handful of rolls, and their stat bonuses are strong enough to carry you through all of the game's content without feeling like you are at a disadvantage.

### Dragonborn (1% Drop Rate)

Dragonborn is a combat-oriented race with a unique twist: fire resistance. In addition to a solid boost to base combat damage, Dragonborn players take reduced damage from fire-based enemies and environmental hazards. This makes Dragonborn particularly valuable in the Forgotten Kingdom and other late-game areas where fire damage is common. The damage bonus is not quite as high as Demon's, but the added survivability from fire resistance more than compensates. Dragonborn also receives a small health boost, making it a durable choice for players who like to push into difficult content without worrying about getting one-shot.

### Vampire (1% Drop Rate)

Vampire is built around lifesteal. Every hit you land in combat heals you for a percentage of the damage dealt, and this percentage is higher than what Demon gets. The result is a race that feels almost impossible to kill as long as you are actively fighting. Vampire players can sustain through encounters that would force other races to retreat and heal. The trade-off is that Vampire has no mining bonuses whatsoever — it is purely a combat race. But if your gameplay revolves around clearing mobs, farming combat drops, or pushing into the hardest zones, Vampire's sustain is hard to beat. At a 1% drop rate, it is rare enough to feel special but not so rare that you will never see it.

### Elf (2% Drop Rate)

Elf is the best mining race in the game, and it is not particularly close. Elf players receive a significant boost to mining speed, a large increase to mining luck (which affects rare ore drop rates), and a small bonus to movement speed. If you spend a lot of time mining — and in The Forge, mining is how you progress through most of the game's crafting and upgrade systems — Elf is the race you want. The mining luck bonus in particular stacks well with [totems](/the-forge-totems-guide) and other luck-boosting items, making Elf players noticeably more efficient at farming rare ores. The combat stats are average, but for a game where mining is central to progression, Elf earns its A-tier placement easily.

## B-Tier Races — Solid Choices for Specific Builds

B-tier races are not as universally strong as the tiers above them, but each one has a clear niche where it performs well. If you roll a B-tier race and it fits your playstyle, there is no urgent need to reroll.

### Orc (3% Drop Rate)

Orc is the straightforward damage race. It provides a noticeable boost to combat damage and a small increase to maximum health, making it a simple but effective choice for players who focus on fighting. Orc does not have any mining bonuses or utility passives — it is purely about hitting harder and surviving a bit longer. For combat-focused players who did not roll an S-tier or A-tier damage race, Orc is a perfectly serviceable alternative. It will not carry you the way Demon or Dragonborn would, but it is a clear step above the common races.

### Dwarf (3% Drop Rate)

Dwarf is the mining counterpart to Orc's combat focus. Dwarf players receive a moderate boost to mining speed and a small increase to ore luck, making them noticeably more efficient at gathering resources. The bonuses are not as large as Elf's, but Dwarf also gets a small health boost that Elf lacks, giving it slightly better survivability in mining areas where mobs can interrupt your farming. If you enjoy the mining side of The Forge and did not roll Elf, Dwarf is a solid consolation prize. Check our [beginner guide](/the-forge-beginner-guide) for tips on making the most of mining bonuses early on.

### Werewolf (4% Drop Rate)

Werewolf is the speed race. It provides a boost to movement speed and a combat damage bonus that increases during nighttime in the game. The night bonus is situational — it only applies during the game's night cycle — but when it is active, Werewolf's damage output rivals some A-tier races. The movement speed bonus is useful in all situations, making traversal between areas faster and giving you a slight edge in dodging enemy attacks. Werewolf is a fun race to play and rewards players who pay attention to the day-night cycle, but its inconsistency keeps it in B-tier.

## C-Tier and D-Tier Races

The remaining races make up the bulk of what most players will roll on their first attempt. They are functional — you can play the game with any race — but they lack the meaningful stat bonuses that make higher-tier races feel impactful.

### C-Tier: Goblin, Undead, Fairy (5% Each)

Goblin provides a small boost to movement speed and a minor increase to gem drops. Undead has a low-level health regeneration passive that helps in early areas but becomes irrelevant once you have better gear. Fairy offers a small mining luck bonus and a tiny movement speed increase. All three of these races are playable and will not hold you back in early or mid-game content, but they are clearly outclassed by everything in B-tier and above. If you are sitting on a C-tier race and you have rerolls available, it is worth trying for something better — but do not panic about it. You can still progress through the game just fine.

### D-Tier: Human, Halfling, Gnome, Troll

These are the races you want to leave behind as quickly as possible. Human is the most common race at a 25% drop rate and provides no passive bonuses at all — it is the baseline that every other race improves upon. Halfling (15%) and Troll (15%) offer negligible stat increases that you will barely notice in gameplay. Gnome (10%) has a very small mining speed bonus, but it is so minor that it does not meaningfully affect your progression. If you are on any D-tier race, use your rerolls. Even landing a C-tier race is a noticeable improvement, and the odds of hitting B-tier or above are reasonable if you have a few rerolls saved up. For more context on how the game works for new players, check the [wiki](/the-forge-wiki).

## How to Reroll Your Race

There are two ways to reroll your race in The Forge, and both are straightforward once you know where to look.

### Method 1: The Wizard NPC

The Wizard is an NPC you can find in two locations: Stonewake's Cross and the Forgotten Kingdom. Walk up to the Wizard and interact with them to open the race reroll menu. From there, you can spend one reroll to randomly assign a new race. The new race follows the same drop rate table shown above — there is no pity system or guaranteed upgrade. You might go from Human to Archangel, or you might go from Human to Halfling. Each roll is independent.

To find the Wizard in Stonewake's Cross, head to the central plaza area near the main crafting stations. The Wizard stands near a glowing purple pedestal that is hard to miss. In the Forgotten Kingdom, the Wizard is located near the entrance portal, slightly off to the right side.

### Method 2: The In-Game Shop

Press T to open the game menu, then navigate to the inventory section. From there, you can access the reroll option directly without needing to visit an NPC. This method uses the same reroll currency and follows the same drop rates — it is simply more convenient because you can do it from anywhere without traveling to a specific location. This is the faster method if you have multiple rerolls to burn and do not want to keep running back to the Wizard between each attempt.

## Reroll Strategy Tips

Knowing when to reroll and when to hold is just as important as knowing the tier list itself. Here are the key principles to follow.

If you are currently on a D-tier race (Human, Halfling, Gnome, or Troll), reroll immediately whenever you have rerolls available. There is no downside — any result is either the same tier or an upgrade. Do not save rerolls "for later" if you are sitting on Human. Use them now.

If you are on a C-tier race (Goblin, Undead, or Fairy), rerolling is still a good idea, but it is less urgent. You have functional passives, and there is a real chance that a reroll lands you on another C-tier or even a D-tier race. If you only have one or two rerolls, you might want to wait until you have three or more so you get multiple attempts in a row. This improves your odds of landing at least one upgrade across the batch.

If you are on a B-tier race, think carefully before rerolling. Orc, Dwarf, and Werewolf are all genuinely useful races that will serve you well through the entire game. The odds of upgrading from B-tier to A-tier or S-tier on any single roll are low — roughly 5.1% combined for all A-tier and S-tier races. Unless you have a large stockpile of rerolls (five or more), it is usually better to keep your B-tier race and save your rerolls for an alt character or a future update that might change the meta.

If you are on an A-tier or S-tier race, never reroll. There is no scenario where gambling away a 1% or 0.1% race makes sense. Lock it in and enjoy the advantage.

One more tip: if you are rerolling multiple times in a row, do them all in one session rather than spreading them out. The odds do not change either way, but doing them together lets you stop as soon as you hit something good rather than accidentally forgetting you had rerolls saved up.

## Race FAQ

### What is the best race in The Forge?

Archangel is the best overall race in the game. It provides top-tier bonuses to combat damage, health regeneration, and mining luck simultaneously. No other race covers all three categories at that level. If you are looking for the best combat-only race, Demon edges out the competition with the highest raw damage bonus.

### Can I have multiple races at the same time?

No. Your character can only have one race active at a time. When you reroll, your current race is completely replaced by the new one. There is no way to store a previous race or switch between races you have already rolled. This is why the decision to reroll should be made carefully — once your current race is gone, it is gone.

### Do races affect mining?

Yes, several races provide mining-related bonuses. Elf has the strongest mining bonuses in the game, including mining speed and mining luck. Dwarf also provides mining bonuses, though they are smaller than Elf's. Archangel includes a mining luck bonus as part of its overall stat package. Most other races either have no mining bonuses or only very minor ones. For a deeper look at how mining works, check the [wiki](/the-forge-wiki).

### How rare is Archangel?

Archangel has a 0.1% drop rate, making it the rarest race in The Forge. Statistically, you would need around 1,000 rerolls to have a roughly 63% chance of seeing it at least once. Many long-time players have never rolled it. If you get Archangel, it is an exceptionally lucky result.

### Can I trade races with other players?

No. Races are bound to your character and cannot be traded, gifted, or transferred in any way. The only way to change your race is through the reroll system using the Wizard NPC or the in-game Shop.

### Do race bonuses stack with totems?

Yes. Race bonuses and [totem](/the-forge-totems-guide) bonuses are separate systems that stack additively. For example, if your race gives you a 10% mining luck bonus and you activate a totem that gives another 15% mining luck, you will have 25% total mining luck. This is why Elf combined with a luck totem is one of the most efficient setups for farming rare ores in the game. For more on how totems work and which ones to prioritize, see our [totems guide](/the-forge-totems-guide).
