## All Quests in The Forge — Complete Guide & Rewards (2026)

Quests are NPC-driven missions scattered across every region of [The Forge](/the-forge-wiki) on Roblox. Unlike simply mining ores and forging weapons on your own, quests give you structured objectives with guaranteed rewards — gold, XP, unique items, and sometimes access to hidden areas or special tools that cannot be obtained any other way. If you have been grinding caves without direction, quests are the fastest way to accelerate your progression and unlock gear that would otherwise take hours of farming to match.

The Forge currently features four distinct regions — Stonewake's Cross, the Forgotten Kingdom, Frostspire Expanse, and Volcanic Depths — and each region has its own set of quest-giving NPCs. Some quests are straightforward kill-or-collect tasks, while others involve finding hidden locations, solving environmental puzzles, or defeating specific bosses under special conditions. A handful of quests are hidden entirely, with no visible quest marker — you have to discover them through exploration or by meeting secret prerequisites.

Quests also chain together. Certain NPCs will not offer their quest until you have completed a prerequisite quest from another NPC. This means the order in which you tackle quests matters, and doing them in the right sequence can save you significant time and give you powerful gear upgrades exactly when you need them. This guide covers every quest in The Forge organized by region, including NPC locations, objectives, rewards, difficulty ratings, and a recommended completion order that chains gear upgrades efficiently. We also link to related guides for [weapons](/the-forge-weapons-guide), [enemies](/the-forge-enemies-guide), [runes](/the-forge-runes-guide), [ores](/the-forge-ores-guide), and [pickaxes](/the-forge-pickaxes-guide) so you can plan your full build path alongside your quest progression.

## How Quests Work

The quest system in The Forge follows a classic RPG structure. NPCs that have a quest available for you display a yellow exclamation mark floating above their head, visible from a moderate distance. Walk up to the NPC and interact with them to open the quest dialogue. The dialogue explains the backstory, the objective, and the reward. Accept the quest and it appears in your quest log, accessible from the menu at any time.

Quest objectives fall into several categories. Collection quests ask you to mine a specific number of ores or gather dropped items from enemies. Kill quests require you to defeat a certain number of a specific enemy type. Delivery quests have you carry items from one NPC to another. Exploration quests send you to find hidden locations, secret caves, or environmental puzzles. Boss quests task you with defeating a specific boss enemy, sometimes with additional conditions like taking no damage from a particular attack or completing the fight solo.

Once you complete the objective, return to the quest-giving NPC. A yellow question mark replaces the exclamation mark, indicating the quest is ready to turn in. Talk to the NPC again to claim your rewards. Gold and XP are deposited immediately. Item rewards appear in your inventory — if your inventory is full, the item drops on the ground near you, so make sure you have space before turning in quests with item rewards.

Some quests are one-time story quests that can only be completed once per account. These tend to offer the best rewards, including unique items and runes that are not available from any other source. Other quests are daily repeatables — they reset every 24 hours and provide a steady income of gold and XP for players who log in regularly. Daily quests are marked with a small clock icon in the quest log. Quest difficulty scales with the region: Stonewake quests are designed for new players, while Volcanic Depths quests assume you have end-game gear and combat experience. Attempting quests above your gear level is possible but significantly harder, and boss quests in particular can be nearly impossible without appropriate [weapons](/the-forge-weapons-guide) and [runes](/the-forge-runes-guide).

## Stonewake's Cross Quests

Stonewake's Cross is the starting region and home to the first NPCs you will encounter. The quests here serve as tutorials for the game's core systems — forging, mining, and combat. Rewards are modest but essential for building your initial loadout. Every new player should complete all four Stonewake quests before moving on, as the gear and XP they provide create a solid foundation for the mid-game.

| Quest | NPC | Objective | Rewards | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| First Forge | Blacksmith Bob | Forge any weapon at the anvil | 50 Gold + 100 XP + Iron Pickaxe | Easy |
| Cave Crawler | Miner Fred | Mine 20 ores of any type | 30 Gold + 75 XP | Easy |
| Zombie Slayer | Guard Captain | Defeat 10 Zombies | 80 Gold + 150 XP + Steel Sword | Easy |
| The Lost Pickaxe | Old Miner | Find the hidden pickaxe in Raven Cave | 100 Gold + 200 XP + Raven Pickaxe | Medium |

Blacksmith Bob stands right next to the main anvil in Stonewake's central plaza. His quest, First Forge, is essentially a tutorial — forge any weapon and he rewards you with an Iron Pickaxe, which is a significant upgrade over the starter pickaxe and lets you mine Silver and Gold ores. Miner Fred is found near the entrance to the first cave. Cave Crawler is a simple collection quest that teaches you the mining loop. The Guard Captain patrols near the town gate and his Zombie Slayer quest introduces combat — the Steel Sword reward is a solid early weapon that carries you through the first two cave tiers.

The Lost Pickaxe is the most interesting Stonewake quest. The Old Miner sits on a bench near the eastern edge of town, and his quest sends you to find a hidden pickaxe inside Raven Cave. The cave entrance is partially concealed behind a rock formation — look for a narrow gap in the wall on the second level of the Stonewake mines. Inside, the Raven Pickaxe sits on a pedestal. This pickaxe has stats comparable to mid-tier purchased [pickaxes](/the-forge-pickaxes-guide) and is completely free, making it one of the best early-game upgrades available.

## Forgotten Kingdom Quests

The Forgotten Kingdom is the second region, accessible after you reach a certain level or complete the Stonewake quest line. The quests here are noticeably harder, with tougher enemies and more demanding objectives. The rewards scale accordingly — you will earn your first rune from this region's quests, which is a game-changing upgrade for combat builds.

| Quest | NPC | Objective | Rewards | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Royal Delivery | Merchant | Deliver 5 Gold Ore to the King | 200 Gold + 300 XP | Medium |
| Skeleton Purge | Knight Commander | Defeat 25 Skeletons | 250 Gold + 400 XP + Mithril Armor | Medium |
| The Bomber Problem | Demolitions Expert | Defeat 10 Bombers without taking explosion damage | 300 Gold + 500 XP + Bomb Rune | Hard |
| Reaper Hunt | Bounty Hunter | Defeat the Reaper mini-boss | 500 Gold + 800 XP + Vampiric Rune | Hard |

Royal Delivery is a straightforward delivery quest. The Merchant NPC stands near the Forgotten Kingdom marketplace and asks you to bring 5 Gold Ore to the King, who sits in the throne room at the far end of the castle. If you have been mining in Stonewake, you likely already have Gold Ore in your inventory — check the [ores guide](/the-forge-ores-guide) for spawn locations. The Knight Commander is stationed at the castle gate and his Skeleton Purge quest is a grind, but the Mithril Armor reward provides a substantial defense boost that makes every subsequent quest easier.

The Bomber Problem is the first quest with a conditional objective. You must defeat 10 Bombers — the explosive [enemies](/the-forge-enemies-guide) that self-destruct when they get close — without taking any explosion damage. This requires either ranged weapons or very precise timing with melee attacks to kill them before they detonate. The Bomb Rune reward adds area-of-effect damage to your attacks, which is useful for clearing groups of weaker enemies.

Reaper Hunt is the standout quest of this region. The Bounty Hunter NPC lurks in a dark alley behind the castle and sends you to defeat the Reaper, a mini-boss that spawns in the deepest part of the Forgotten Kingdom caves. The Reaper hits hard and has a life-drain attack, but defeating it earns you the Vampiric Rune — one of the strongest [runes](/the-forge-runes-guide) in the game. This rune heals you for a percentage of damage dealt, effectively giving you sustain in every fight from this point forward.

## Frostspire Expanse Quests

Frostspire Expanse is the third region, a frozen landscape filled with ice caves, crystal formations, and some of the toughest non-boss enemies in the game. The quests here demand solid gear and combat skill. Crystal Spiders, Yetis, and Crystal Golems are all formidable opponents, and the quest objectives reflect that difficulty.

| Quest | NPC | Objective | Rewards | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Spider Silk Collection | Weaver | Collect 15 Spider Silk from Crystal Spiders | 400 Gold + 600 XP | Medium |
| Yeti Slayer | Frost Warden | Defeat the Yeti boss | 800 Gold + 1200 XP + Frostite Pickaxe | Hard |
| The Hidden Maze | Explorer | Find and complete the Hidden Maze | 600 Gold + 1000 XP + Maze Pickaxe | Hard |
| Crystal Golem Challenge | Arena Master | Defeat the Crystal Golem solo | 1000 Gold + 1500 XP + Thorns Rune | Very Hard |

Spider Silk Collection is a farming quest. Crystal Spiders drop Spider Silk on defeat, but the drop rate is not 100%, so expect to kill 25-30 spiders to collect 15 silk. The Weaver NPC is found in a small hut near the Frostspire village entrance. This quest has no special reward item but provides good gold and XP for the effort involved. Pair it with general Frostspire mining runs to maximize efficiency.

Yeti Slayer is a boss quest given by the Frost Warden, who guards the path to the Frostspire summit. The Yeti is a large, slow boss with devastating area attacks. It slams the ground to create ice shockwaves and throws boulders at range. The key strategy is to stay at medium range, dodge the shockwaves by jumping, and attack during the recovery animation after each slam. The Frostite Pickaxe reward is one of the best [pickaxes](/the-forge-pickaxes-guide) in the game, capable of mining every ore type in the Frostspire region at high speed.

The Hidden Maze is an exploration quest. The Explorer NPC sits at a campfire near the eastern ice cliffs and tells you about a maze hidden beneath the glacier. The entrance is a cracked ice wall that you must break with any pickaxe — it is located in the lower section of the Frostspire caves, behind a waterfall of ice. Inside, the maze is a series of branching tunnels with dead ends, traps, and a few Crystal Spider spawns. Reaching the center rewards you with the Maze Pickaxe, a unique tool with a built-in ore detection ability.

Crystal Golem Challenge is the hardest quest in Frostspire. The Arena Master stands outside a sealed arena door near the summit and will only open it if you agree to fight the Crystal Golem alone — no group members allowed. The Crystal Golem has extremely high HP, reflects a portion of melee damage back at you, and periodically summons smaller crystal minions. You need strong [weapons](/the-forge-weapons-guide) and the Vampiric Rune from Reaper Hunt to sustain through this fight. The Thorns Rune reward reflects damage back at enemies that hit you, which is incredibly powerful for tanking in group content.

## Volcanic Depths Quests

Volcanic Depths is the final region and home to the most challenging content in The Forge. The quests here assume you have completed most of the previous regions and have end-game gear. Rewards include massive gold payouts, the highest XP gains in the game, and some of the rarest runes available.

| Quest | NPC | Objective | Rewards | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Infernal Harvest | Volcanic Smith | Mine 10 Infernal Ore | 1500 Gold + 2000 XP | Hard |
| Demon Lord | War Chief | Defeat the Volcanic Depths final boss | 3000 Gold + 5000 XP + Annihilation Rune | Very Hard |
| The Arcane Secret | Mysterious NPC | Find Arcane Crystal + complete puzzle | 2000 Gold + 3000 XP + Mother Lode Rune | Very Hard |

Infernal Harvest is given by the Volcanic Smith, who works at a forge near the entrance to the Volcanic Depths caves. Mining 10 Infernal Ore sounds simple, but Infernal Ore nodes are rare, spawn deep in lava-adjacent tunnels, and are guarded by Lava Golems and Fire Elementals. You need a high-tier [pickaxe](/the-forge-pickaxes-guide) to mine them efficiently and strong combat gear to survive the enemies nearby. This quest is a good warm-up for the region and the gold reward is substantial.

Demon Lord is the climactic quest of The Forge. The War Chief stands at the entrance to the final boss arena and sends you to defeat the Demon Lord, the most powerful enemy in the game. This boss has multiple phases, each with different attack patterns — fire breath, ground slams, summoned minions, and a devastating charge attack. Group play is strongly recommended, though solo completion is possible with perfect gear and the Vampiric Rune. The Annihilation Rune reward massively boosts your damage output, making it the best offensive rune in the game. Check the [enemies guide](/the-forge-enemies-guide) for a detailed breakdown of the Demon Lord's attack patterns.

The Arcane Secret is a hidden quest — there is no quest marker. The Mysterious NPC is concealed behind a breakable wall in the deepest section of the Volcanic Depths. To find them, look for a wall segment that appears slightly different in color from its surroundings, near the lava river on the lowest level. Break through it with your pickaxe to reveal a hidden chamber. The Mysterious NPC asks you to bring an Arcane Crystal — the rarest ore in the game — and then complete a pattern-matching puzzle on a pedestal. The Mother Lode Rune reward increases all ore drop rates, making every mining run significantly more productive. This is arguably the most valuable rune for long-term progression.

## Recommended Quest Order

Completing quests in the right order chains gear upgrades together so each new piece of equipment arrives exactly when you need it for the next challenge. Here is the optimal path:

1. First Forge, then Cave Crawler, then Zombie Slayer — these three Stonewake quests give you the Iron Pickaxe and Steel Sword, which are your baseline gear for everything that follows. Complete them in this order because the Iron Pickaxe from First Forge makes Cave Crawler faster, and the Steel Sword from Zombie Slayer prepares you for combat quests.

2. The Lost Pickaxe — do this immediately after the Stonewake basics. The Raven Pickaxe is free and has stats that rival mid-tier purchased pickaxes. It will carry your mining through the entire Forgotten Kingdom region without needing an upgrade.

3. Royal Delivery, then Skeleton Purge — these Forgotten Kingdom quests are straightforward and the Mithril Armor from Skeleton Purge is a critical defensive upgrade before you attempt the harder quests.

4. The Bomber Problem — get the Bomb Rune for area damage. This makes clearing groups of enemies much faster and helps with the grind quests ahead.

5. Reaper Hunt — this is the most important single quest in the game. The Vampiric Rune transforms your combat capability by adding life steal to every attack. With this rune equipped, you can sustain through fights that would otherwise require constant healing item usage.

6. Spider Silk Collection, then Yeti Slayer, then The Hidden Maze — progress through Frostspire in this order. The Frostite Pickaxe from Yeti Slayer and the Maze Pickaxe from The Hidden Maze are both excellent tools for the late game.

7. Crystal Golem Challenge — attempt this only after you have the Vampiric Rune and strong Frostspire weapons. The Thorns Rune reward is powerful but the fight is brutal without proper preparation.

8. Infernal Harvest, then Demon Lord, then The Arcane Secret — end-game quests in the Volcanic Depths. The Annihilation Rune from Demon Lord and the Mother Lode Rune from The Arcane Secret are the ultimate rewards in The Forge.

This order works because each reward directly enables the next quest. The Raven Pickaxe lets you mine efficiently in the Forgotten Kingdom. The Vampiric Rune lets you survive Frostspire combat. The Thorns Rune and Frostite Pickaxe prepare you for the Volcanic Depths. Skipping ahead is possible but significantly harder.

## Hidden Quests

Not every quest in The Forge announces itself with a floating exclamation mark. Three quests are hidden and require specific actions to discover.

The Lost Pickaxe in Stonewake's Cross has a visible NPC (the Old Miner) but the quest objective location is hidden. The Raven Cave entrance is concealed behind a rock formation on the second level of the Stonewake mines. Most players walk right past it. Look for a narrow gap between two large boulders on the eastern wall — you can squeeze through to enter a short tunnel that leads to the Raven Cave. The pickaxe sits on a glowing pedestal at the back of the cave.

The Bomber Problem in the Forgotten Kingdom has a secret completion condition that the quest text does not fully explain. The quest says to defeat 10 Bombers without taking explosion damage, but there is an additional hidden requirement: you must defeat all 10 in a single session without dying. If you die and respawn, the counter resets to zero. This makes the quest significantly harder than it appears, especially for melee builds. Ranged [weapons](/the-forge-weapons-guide) like the Silver Shortbow or Crystal Bow make this quest much more manageable.

The Arcane Secret in the Volcanic Depths is the most thoroughly hidden quest in the game. There is no quest marker, no NPC visible on the map, and no dialogue hint from other NPCs. The Mysterious NPC is behind a breakable wall in the deepest section of the Volcanic Depths — the wall looks slightly different in texture and color compared to the surrounding rock. Break it with any pickaxe to reveal the hidden chamber. The quest itself requires an Arcane Crystal, which is the rarest [ore](/the-forge-ores-guide) in the game with an extremely low spawn rate in the Volcanic Depths caves. Many players farm for hours before finding one. Once you have the crystal, the NPC presents a pattern-matching puzzle on a stone pedestal — memorize the sequence of glowing symbols and repeat it correctly to complete the quest.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I repeat quests?**
Story quests — the main quests listed in this guide — can only be completed once per account. However, several NPCs offer daily repeatable quests that reset every 24 hours. Daily quests are simpler versions of the story quests (mine X ores, defeat Y enemies) and provide smaller but consistent gold and XP rewards. Look for NPCs with a blue exclamation mark instead of yellow — those are daily quest givers.

**What is the best quest for earning gold?**
For a one-time payout, Demon Lord gives 3000 gold, the highest single reward in the game. For repeatable income, the daily quests in the Volcanic Depths region offer the best gold-per-completion ratio. If you are still in the early game, completing all four Stonewake quests gives a combined 260 gold, which is enough to buy your first meaningful gear upgrades.

**Do I need to complete quests in a specific order?**
Some quests have prerequisites — for example, certain Forgotten Kingdom NPCs will not offer their quests until you have completed the Stonewake quest line. Others are independent and can be completed in any order. The recommended order in this guide is optimized for gear progression, not strict requirements. You can deviate from it, but you may find some quests harder without the gear from earlier ones.

**What happens if I fail a quest?**
There is no penalty for failing a quest. If you die during a boss quest, you respawn and can try again immediately. Kill and collection quest progress is saved even if you die — you do not lose your counter. The only exception is The Bomber Problem, which has a hidden requirement to complete all kills in a single life.

**Are there group quests?**
There are no quests that strictly require a group, but boss quests like Yeti Slayer, Crystal Golem Challenge, and Demon Lord are significantly easier with teammates. The Crystal Golem Challenge is the exception — it explicitly requires solo completion. For all other boss quests, grouping up is the recommended approach, especially if your gear is below the ideal level for the region.

**Where is the Mysterious NPC?**
The Mysterious NPC is hidden behind a breakable wall in the deepest section of the Volcanic Depths. Navigate to the lowest level of the Volcanic Depths caves, near the lava river. Look for a wall segment that has a slightly different color or texture compared to the surrounding rock. Hit it with your pickaxe to break through and reveal the hidden chamber. The NPC is inside, waiting with The Arcane Secret quest. You will need an Arcane Crystal in your inventory to accept the quest.
