## Pickaxe Stats Explained

Before diving into the full pickaxe list, it is important to understand what each stat actually does and why it matters. The Forge uses four stats on every pickaxe, and they each serve a distinct purpose in your mining gameplay.

**Mine Power** is the most fundamental stat. It determines which ores you are able to mine. Every ore in the game has a minimum power requirement, and if your pickaxe does not meet that threshold, you simply cannot break the ore vein. For example, early ores like Copper and Tin only require 10 to 15 Mine Power, but late-game ores like Demonite and Arcane Crystal demand 200 or more. This means upgrading your Mine Power is not optional — it is a hard gate on your progression. Check our [Ores Guide](/the-forge-ores-guide) for the exact power requirements of every ore.

**Mining Speed** is a percentage modifier that controls how fast you break ore veins. A pickaxe with +50% Mining Speed will mine ores roughly 1.5 times faster than the base Stone Pickaxe. This stat matters most when you are farming large quantities of ore for crafting or selling. The speed difference between a 0% pickaxe and a +80% pickaxe is enormous over a long mining session, so do not underestimate this stat even if your Mine Power is already high enough for the ores you need.

**Luck Boost** increases your chance of receiving rare ore drops when you mine. This stacks additively with other luck sources in the game, including the Luck Totem consumable (which you can get for free from [codes](/the-forge-codes)) and certain race bonuses. If you are farming for rare ores like Prismatic Shards or Void Essence, a high Luck Boost pickaxe combined with a Luck Totem can dramatically improve your drop rates.

**Rune Slots** determine how many runes you can attach to your pickaxe. Runes provide additional passive bonuses like extra speed, extra luck, or special effects. A pickaxe with 5 Rune Slots gives you far more customization than one with 0 or 1 slot. Rune Slots cannot be added or upgraded — they are fixed to the pickaxe itself. See our [Runes Guide](/the-forge-runes-guide) for the best rune combinations for each pickaxe tier.

## Pickaxe Tier List

This tier list ranks every pickaxe based on overall value, considering stats, cost efficiency, and availability. A pickaxe in S-Tier is not just strong — it represents the best possible option for its stage of the game or offers a unique advantage that no other pickaxe can match.

### S-Tier — Best in Game

- **Prismatic Pickaxe** — The undisputed king. 300 Mine Power lets you mine every ore in the game, +80% Speed makes farming incredibly fast, and +40% Luck with 5 Rune Slots means you can stack luck runes for insane rare drop rates. If you can afford the 75,000 Gold price tag, this is the endgame pickaxe.
- **Dragon Head Pickaxe** — Nearly as strong as Prismatic at a lower price point. 250 Mine Power covers almost all ores, and the 5 Rune Slots give you full build flexibility. This is the realistic endgame target for most players.
- **Maze Pickaxe** — A hidden gem, literally. 220 Mine Power with +65% Speed and 5 Rune Slots, and it costs nothing. The only barrier is finding and completing the Hidden Maze in Volcanic Depths. If you can get it, this pickaxe outperforms everything in its price range because its price is zero.

### A-Tier — Strong Late-Game Options

- **Demonic Pickaxe** — 200 Mine Power is the threshold for mining Demonite, and the +60% Speed with 4 Rune Slots makes this a solid workhorse for Volcanic Depths farming.
- **Frostite Pickaxe** — The best pickaxe in Frostspire. 170 Mine Power handles everything in the region, and +55% Speed keeps your farming efficient.
- **Raven Pickaxe** — Free and hidden in Frostspire. At 180 Mine Power it actually outperforms the Frostite Pickaxe in raw power, though it has slightly lower speed. Excellent value.
- **Sakura Pickaxe** — If you have it from the event, the +35% Luck at only 160 Mine Power makes it the best luck-farming pickaxe until you reach Volcanic Depths tier.

### B-Tier — Solid Mid-to-Late Progression

- **Prismarine Pickaxe** — 150 Mine Power with 4 Rune Slots is a strong combination. This is where your build options really start to open up.
- **Diamond Pickaxe** — A reliable step up from Crystal with +45% Speed and +18% Luck. Good for extended Frostspire farming.
- **Crystal Pickaxe** — Your entry ticket to Frostspire mining. 110 Mine Power unlocks the region's basic ores, and 3 Rune Slots let you run a decent rune setup.

### C-Tier — Mid-Game Workhorses

- **Obsidian Pickaxe** — The bridge between Forgotten Kingdom and Frostspire. 95 Mine Power with 3 Rune Slots is serviceable but you will want to upgrade quickly.
- **Mithril Pickaxe** — Solid mid-game option with 2 Rune Slots and +10% Luck. Good value at 3,000 Gold.
- **Platinum Pickaxe** — A decent upgrade over Gold if you have the gold to spare, but Mithril is not much more expensive and significantly better.
- **Gold Pickaxe** — Your first 2-slot pickaxe. This is where rune builds start to matter.

### D-Tier — Early Game Only

- **Steel Pickaxe** — Functional in Stonewake but outclassed quickly. Skip this if you can save for Gold Pickaxe.
- **Iron Pickaxe** — Your first rune slot. Worth buying early for the slot alone, but replace it as soon as you reach Forgotten Kingdom.
- **Copper Pickaxe** — Marginal upgrade over Stone. Only buy this if you need the +5% Speed for early farming.
- **Stone Pickaxe** — Everyone starts here. Replace it as soon as possible.

## Recommended Upgrade Path

Knowing which pickaxe to buy next saves you gold and keeps your progression smooth. Here is the optimal upgrade path from start to endgame.

**Starting out (0-200 Gold):** You begin with the Stone Pickaxe. Your first priority is earning enough gold to buy the **Iron Pickaxe** for 150 Gold in Stonewake. The Iron Pickaxe is the most important early upgrade because it unlocks your first Rune Slot, gives you +10% Speed, and has enough Mine Power (25) to handle all Stonewake ores comfortably. Skip the Copper Pickaxe entirely — the 50 Gold is better saved toward Iron.

**Early mid-game (200-1,000 Gold):** Once you have the Iron Pickaxe, farm Stonewake ores until you can afford the **Gold Pickaxe** for 800 Gold in Forgotten Kingdom. You can skip the Steel Pickaxe — it only adds 10 Mine Power over Iron and does not give you an extra Rune Slot. The Gold Pickaxe jumps you to 50 Mine Power, +20% Speed, +5% Luck, and 2 Rune Slots, which is a massive leap.

**Mid-game (1,000-8,000 Gold):** In Forgotten Kingdom, work toward the **Mithril Pickaxe** at 3,000 Gold. Platinum is fine as a stepping stone if you need the power, but Mithril's +10% Luck and 80 Mine Power make it the better target. From Mithril, save for the **Crystal Pickaxe** at 8,000 Gold to unlock Frostspire mining.

**Late-game (8,000-50,000 Gold):** In Frostspire, the **Diamond Pickaxe** at 12,000 Gold is a solid upgrade, but if you can find the **Raven Pickaxe** in Raven Cave (see Hidden Pickaxe Locations below), you can skip Diamond entirely and save thousands of gold. Otherwise, progress through Diamond to **Frostite** at 25,000 Gold before heading to Volcanic Depths.

**Endgame (50,000+ Gold):** In Volcanic Depths, the **Dragon Head Pickaxe** at 50,000 Gold is your realistic endgame target. If you want the absolute best, save for the **Prismatic Pickaxe** at 75,000 Gold. Also check the Hidden Maze for the free **Maze Pickaxe** — it sits between Demonic and Dragon Head in power and costs nothing.

## Hidden Pickaxe Locations

Two of the best pickaxes in The Forge are completely hidden and free to obtain. Here is exactly how to find them.

### Raven Pickaxe — Raven Cave (Frostspire)

The Raven Cave is located in the Frostspire Expanse region. To find it, head to the third mining chamber — the large cavern with the blue crystal formations on the ceiling. Along the right wall of this chamber, look for a section of rock that has visible cracks running through it. These cracks are subtle but distinct from the normal cave wall texture. Mine the cracked wall to break through it, revealing a hidden passage.

Follow the passage through a narrow tunnel that curves to the left. You will emerge in a small circular cave with a stone pedestal in the center. The **Raven Pickaxe** sits on this pedestal. Walk up to it and interact to claim it. The Raven Pickaxe has 180 Mine Power, +50% Speed, +28% Luck, and 4 Rune Slots — making it stronger than the Frostite Pickaxe that costs 25,000 Gold. This is one of the best-kept secrets in the game.

### Maze Pickaxe — Hidden Maze (Volcanic Depths)

The Hidden Maze entrance is in Volcanic Depths, near the lava river crossing. Look for a small gap in the rock wall on the eastern side of the main mining area. The gap is partially obscured by steam vents, so you may need to adjust your camera angle to spot it. Walk through the gap to enter the maze.

The maze consists of branching corridors with lava pits as dead ends. The solution pattern is **left, right, left, right** at each fork. Follow this pattern consistently and you will reach the center of the maze in about 60 seconds. At the center, the **Maze Pickaxe** rests on a volcanic stone altar. Interact with it to claim it. With 220 Mine Power, +65% Speed, +32% Luck, and 5 Rune Slots, the Maze Pickaxe is stronger than the Demonic Pickaxe and rivals the Dragon Head Pickaxe — all for free.

Both hidden pickaxes are permanent once claimed. They do not expire and they do not require any quest completion. You simply need to find them and pick them up.

## Pickaxe and Rune Combinations

Runes add passive bonuses to your pickaxe, and the right combination can dramatically boost your mining efficiency. Here are the best rune setups for different playstyles.

**Speed farming build:** Equip Mining Speed runes on a high-luck pickaxe like the Prismatic or Sakura. This way you get fast mining from runes while your pickaxe's natural luck stat handles rare drops. On a 5-slot pickaxe, five Mining Speed runes can push your total speed modifier well above +100%.

**Luck farming build:** Equip Luck runes on a high-speed pickaxe like the Maze Pickaxe or Dragon Head. The pickaxe's natural speed keeps your mining fast while stacked Luck runes maximize your rare ore drop chance. This is the best setup for farming Arcane Crystal and other ultra-rare ores.

**Fortune build:** The Fortune rune is a special rune that increases the quantity of ore you receive per vein. Stack Fortune runes on any endgame pickaxe for maximum ore yield per mining session. This is ideal for bulk farming common ores for crafting or selling.

**Balanced build:** Mix one or two of each rune type for a well-rounded setup. This works best on 4 or 5 slot pickaxes where you have room to diversify. A typical balanced build might be 2 Speed runes, 2 Luck runes, and 1 Fortune rune.

## The Forge Pickaxes FAQ

**What is the best pickaxe in The Forge?**
The Prismatic Pickaxe is the best pickaxe by raw stats — 300 Mine Power, +80% Speed, +40% Luck, and 5 Rune Slots. However, the Maze Pickaxe is arguably the best value pickaxe in the game because it offers 220 Mine Power with 5 Rune Slots for free. If you factor in cost efficiency, the Maze Pickaxe wins.

**Can I sell pickaxes in The Forge?**
No. Pickaxes are permanent once purchased or claimed. You cannot sell them, trade them, or drop them. Every pickaxe you buy stays in your inventory forever, so there is no penalty for buying intermediate pickaxes on your way to endgame.

**Do pickaxes break or have durability?**
No. All pickaxes in The Forge have infinite durability. Once you own a pickaxe, you can use it forever without any repair costs or degradation. This is different from weapons, which can break during combat.

**How do I unlock more rune slots on my pickaxe?**
You cannot add rune slots to an existing pickaxe. Rune Slots are a fixed stat that comes with the pickaxe when you buy or find it. The only way to get more Rune Slots is to upgrade to a pickaxe that has more slots built in. The earliest pickaxe with a rune slot is the Iron Pickaxe (1 slot), and the maximum is 5 slots on the Dragon Head, Prismatic, and Maze pickaxes.

**Where is Miner Fred?**
Miner Fred is the NPC who sells pickaxes. He has a shop in every major region: Stonewake, Forgotten Kingdom, Frostspire, and Volcanic Depths. Each shop sells the pickaxes available for that region. Look for his anvil icon on the minimap.

**Are limited-time pickaxes better than regular ones?**
Limited pickaxes like the Sakura Pickaxe have unique stat distributions that regular pickaxes do not offer. The Sakura Pickaxe, for example, has +35% Luck at only 160 Mine Power — no regular pickaxe at that power level comes close to that luck value. However, limited pickaxes are not strictly better across all stats. They tend to specialize in one area (usually Luck) at the cost of lower Mine Power or Speed compared to regular pickaxes at the same tier.

**Can I use multiple pickaxes at once?**
No. You can only equip one pickaxe at a time. However, you can switch between pickaxes freely from your inventory, so you can keep a high-luck pickaxe for rare ore farming and a high-speed pickaxe for bulk mining and swap between them as needed.
