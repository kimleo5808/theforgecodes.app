## The Forge Runes Guide — All Runes & Best Builds (2026)

Runes are enhancement items in [The Forge](/the-forge-wiki) that you attach to your gear — weapons, pickaxes, and armor — to add special effects and passive abilities. They are the primary way to customize your build beyond raw stats. A well-runed pickaxe mines faster and pulls rarer ores. A well-runed weapon hits harder, heals you on contact, or triggers devastating critical strikes. A well-runed set of armor can turn you into an unkillable tank that reflects damage back at attackers. Without runes, your gear is just base stats. With the right runes, your gear becomes a build.

Every piece of gear in The Forge has a limited number of Rune Slots determined by its quality tier. You obtain runes through enemy drops, quest rewards, NPC shops, boss kills, and hidden quests scattered across the game world. Runes themselves come in five rarity tiers — Common, Uncommon, Rare, Epic, and Legendary — with higher rarity runes providing significantly stronger effects. Some runes are universal and can be attached to any gear type, while others are restricted to weapons only, pickaxes only, or armor only.

This guide covers every rune currently available in The Forge, explains how the rune system works, lists the best build combinations for mining, combat, tank, and hybrid playstyles, and tells you exactly where to find each rune. Whether you are a new player trying to understand what runes do or a veteran min-maxing your endgame loadout, this page has you covered. For related gear information, check the [weapons guide](/the-forge-weapons-guide), [pickaxes guide](/the-forge-pickaxes-guide), and [enemies guide](/the-forge-enemies-guide) for drop sources.

## How Runes Work

The rune system in The Forge is straightforward once you understand the basics. Each piece of gear — whether it is a weapon, pickaxe, or armor — has a set number of rune slots. The number of slots depends entirely on the gear's quality tier, which is determined during the forging minigame. Common quality gear has zero to one rune slots. Uncommon gear has one slot. Rare gear has one to two slots. Epic gear has two to three slots. Legendary gear has three to four slots. This means that forging high-quality gear is not just about higher base stats — it also unlocks more rune slots for customization.

To attach a rune to a piece of gear, you need to visit the Rune Table NPC. This NPC is located in Stonewake's Cross near the main anvil area, and additional Rune Tables can be found in the Forgotten Kingdom hub and the Frostspire outpost. Interact with the Rune Table, select the gear piece you want to modify, and then drag a rune from your inventory into an empty slot. The rune takes effect immediately — there is no crafting minigame or waiting period.

There is one critical rule to understand: runes can be removed from gear, but the rune is destroyed in the process. You get the slot back, but the rune itself is gone. This means you should think carefully before attaching a rare or legendary rune to a piece of gear you might replace soon. A common strategy is to save your best runes for Epic or Legendary quality gear that you plan to use long-term, and use Common or Uncommon runes on transitional gear that you will eventually outgrow.

Runes have five rarity tiers that directly correspond to their power level. Common runes provide small, straightforward bonuses like a flat damage increase or a minor speed boost. Uncommon runes offer stronger versions of those effects or combine two smaller bonuses into one slot. Rare runes introduce unique mechanics like lifesteal, damage reflection, or stamina cost reduction that fundamentally change how your gear performs. Epic runes are powerful build-defining effects that often have conditional triggers — for example, a massive damage boost that only activates when your HP drops below a threshold. Legendary runes are the rarest and most impactful, offering game-changing abilities like automatic revival or guaranteed double ore drops.

Some runes are type-restricted. Weapon-only runes like Sharpness and Fury can only be attached to swords, axes, bows, and other weapons. Pickaxe-only runes like Haste and Fortune only work on pickaxes. Armor-only runes like Vitality and Guardian only attach to armor pieces. A few runes are universal and can go on any gear type, though these are uncommon. Always check the rune's type tag before planning your build.

## Best Rune Builds

Runes are most effective when you build around a specific playstyle rather than spreading bonuses randomly across your gear. Here are the four strongest rune builds in the current meta, each optimized for a different way of playing The Forge.

### Mining Build

The mining build maximizes your ore output and mining efficiency. This is the build for players who spend most of their time in caves farming resources.

On your pickaxe, the core combination is Fortune + Efficiency + Haste. Fortune increases your rare ore drop chance by 10%, Efficiency boosts mining speed by 20% while reducing stamina cost by 10%, and Haste adds another 8% mining speed on top. Together, these three runes make you mine roughly 28% faster while finding significantly more rare ores per session. If your pickaxe has an Epic or Legendary slot available, replace Haste with Prospector — the +25% rare ore chance and +15% luck from Prospector far outweigh the 8% speed from Haste. The ultimate mining pickaxe runs Fortune + Efficiency + Prospector + Mother Lode on a Legendary pickaxe, giving you absurd rare ore rates and a chance to double every ore you mine.

For armor, run Swiftness to move between ore nodes faster, and Endurance for the stamina regeneration that keeps you mining without breaks. Vitality is a solid filler if you have extra slots.

### Combat DPS Build

The DPS build is designed to kill enemies and bosses as fast as possible. This is the build for players who farm [enemies](/the-forge-enemies-guide) for drops and push into the hardest content.

On your weapon, start with Sharpness + Fury as your base — that is +5% damage and +12% damage with +5% attack speed, a reliable foundation. Add Vampiric in the third slot for 3% lifesteal, which provides sustain during long fights without needing to heal. For boss encounters where you expect to take heavy damage, swap Sharpness for Berserker — the +25% damage boost below 50% HP is enormous and synergizes perfectly with Vampiric's lifesteal to keep you hovering in the danger zone. The endgame DPS weapon runs Fury + Vampiric + Berserker + Annihilation on a Legendary weapon, combining raw damage, crit chance, lifesteal, and the low-HP damage multiplier into a devastating package.

For armor, pair Regeneration with Guardian to stay alive while dealing damage. The +5 HP per second from Regeneration combined with Vampiric lifesteal gives you strong passive healing.

### Tank Build

The tank build prioritizes survival above all else. This is the build for players who want to absorb damage in group content or survive solo in the Volcanic Depths where enemies hit extremely hard.

On your armor, the core is Vitality + Guardian + Thorns. Vitality adds +50 max HP, Guardian provides +15% damage reduction, and Thorns reflects 10% of incoming damage back at attackers. This combination makes you significantly harder to kill while punishing enemies for hitting you. If you have Epic or Legendary armor slots, add Regeneration for +5 HP per second of passive healing and Titan for the massive +200 HP and +20% damage reduction. A fully runed Legendary armor set with Vitality + Guardian + Thorns + Regeneration + Titan turns you into a walking fortress.

On your weapon, run Vampiric for lifesteal sustain and Fury for enough damage to still kill things at a reasonable pace. Tanks do not need to maximize damage — they need to not die.

### Balanced / Hybrid Build

The hybrid build is for players who do a bit of everything — mining, combat, questing — and do not want to swap gear constantly. This is the most practical build for general gameplay.

On your pickaxe, run Fortune + Haste. You skip Efficiency to save the slot for a more impactful rune later, but Fortune and Haste together give you solid mining performance without committing fully to a mining build. On your weapon, run Sharpness + Fury + Vampiric — the same reliable DPS core that works in every situation. On your armor, run Vitality + Guardian for a balance of HP and damage reduction. This loadout handles every piece of content in the game competently. You will not mine as fast as a dedicated mining build or kill as fast as a dedicated DPS build, but you can do both without returning to town to swap runes. For players working through the [quests guide](/the-forge-quests-guide) content, this is often the most efficient approach.

## How to Get Runes

Runes come from six primary sources in The Forge, each associated with different rarity tiers.

Enemy drops are the most common source. Every enemy in the game has a chance to drop Common and Uncommon runes when killed. The drop rate varies by enemy type and region — tougher enemies in later regions have higher drop rates and can occasionally drop Rare runes. Standard cave enemies in Stonewake drop Common runes at roughly a 5% rate per kill. Enemies in the Forgotten Kingdom drop Common runes at 8% and Uncommon runes at 3%. Frostspire enemies drop up to Rare runes at low rates. Farming enemy drops is a passive way to accumulate runes — you will naturally collect dozens of Common runes just by playing through the game's combat content. Check the [enemies guide](/the-forge-enemies-guide) for specific enemy locations and drop tables.

Quest rewards are the primary source of Uncommon and some Rare runes. Many quests in The Forge reward a specific rune upon completion. The Stonewake questline rewards Fury and Guardian. The Forgotten Kingdom questline rewards Fortune and Precision. These are guaranteed drops — complete the quest, get the rune. The [quests guide](/the-forge-quests-guide) lists every quest reward including rune drops.

The NPC shop sells select runes for gold. The Forgotten Kingdom shop sells Endurance for 2000 Gold. The Frostspire shop sells Efficiency for 5000 Gold. Shop runes are always available and provide a reliable way to get specific Rare runes without farming. Prices are steep but fair for the power level.

Boss drops are the primary source of Rare and Epic runes. Each major boss in the game has one or two specific runes in its loot table. The Reaper in the Forgotten Kingdom drops Vampiric. The Crystal Golem in Frostspire drops Thorns. The Yeti boss drops Berserker. The Volcanic Depths bosses drop Regeneration, Executioner, and Annihilation. Boss rune drop rates range from 10% to 25% depending on the boss and rune rarity, so expect to farm each boss multiple times.

Hidden quests are the source of some of the best Epic and Legendary runes. Prospector comes from a hidden quest chain in the Forgotten Kingdom that requires finding three buried tablets. Mother Lode requires completing a secret quest chain that spans all four regions. These quests are not marked on the map — you need to discover them through exploration or community guides.

Secret areas occasionally contain rune chests that guarantee a rune drop of Rare or higher quality. These areas are hidden behind puzzles, destructible walls, or require specific items to access. The Frostspire has a secret merchant behind an ice wall who sells Unbreaking for a special currency.

## Rune Slot Mechanics

The number of rune slots on a piece of gear is determined by its quality tier, which is set during the forging minigame. Understanding slot counts is essential for planning your builds because a gear piece with more slots can hold more runes and therefore more power.

Common quality gear has zero to one rune slots. Most Common gear has zero slots, making runes irrelevant for this tier. Occasionally a Common piece will roll one slot, but this is uncommon. Uncommon quality gear always has exactly one rune slot. This is the minimum viable tier for rune builds — one slot is enough for a single impactful rune like Fortune on a pickaxe or Vampiric on a weapon. Rare quality gear has one to two rune slots. Two slots allow you to start combining runes for synergy, like pairing Fury with Sharpness on a weapon. Epic quality gear has two to three rune slots. Three slots is where builds really come together — you can run a full core combination like Fortune + Efficiency + Haste on a pickaxe. Legendary quality gear has three to four rune slots. Four slots is the maximum and allows you to stack the most powerful rune combinations in the game.

The quality tier is entirely determined by your performance in the forging minigame. Better timing on the anvil strikes means higher quality, which means more rune slots. This creates a direct link between player skill and build potential — a player who consistently forges Legendary gear has access to four-slot items that can hold dramatically more powerful rune combinations than someone forging Rare gear with only two slots.

Both guides list base stats by quality tier so you can plan which gear pieces deserve your best runes.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I remove runes from gear?

Yes, you can remove runes at the Rune Table NPC. However, the rune is destroyed when removed — you get the empty slot back but lose the rune permanently. This is intentional to prevent players from freely swapping runes between gear pieces. Always think carefully before attaching a valuable rune.

### Can runes stack?

You cannot place two copies of the same rune on one piece of gear — each rune slot must contain a different rune. However, similar effects from different runes do stack. For example, Sharpness (+5% damage) and Fury (+12% damage) both increase damage and their bonuses stack additively for +17% total damage on that weapon.

### What is the best rune in the game?

It depends on your playstyle. For mining, Mother Lode is the strongest single rune — the +40% rare ore chance combined with a double ore proc is unmatched. For combat, Annihilation is the most raw power with +40% damage and +10% crit chance. For survival, Immortal is arguably the best because an automatic revive at 50% HP can save a boss run. There is no single "best" rune — the best rune is the one that fits your build.

### Do runes break or expire?

No. Once a rune is attached to gear, it lasts forever as long as the gear exists. Runes do not have durability, charges, or expiration timers. The only way to lose a rune is by removing it from gear (which destroys it) or by losing the gear piece itself.

### Can I trade runes with other players?

Yes, runes can be traded between players through the trade window. This makes rare and legendary runes valuable trade commodities. Many players farm specific bosses to collect duplicate runes for trading. Common and Uncommon runes have low trade value, but Rare runes and above are always in demand.

### Where should I use my first runes?

New players should attach their first runes to whatever gear they plan to use for a while. If you just forged a Rare quality Iron Broadsword and do not plan to upgrade for several hours, put a Sharpness rune on it. Do not hoard Common runes waiting for perfect gear — they drop frequently enough that you will always find more. Save your Rare and above runes for Epic or Legendary gear.
