## All Weapons in The Forge — Crafting Guide & Tier List (2026)

Weapons are the heart of [The Forge](/the-forge-wiki). Every piece of gear you wield started as raw ore pulled from a cave wall, carried to the anvil, and hammered into shape through the forging minigame. The Forge is a blacksmithing RPG on Roblox where the entire progression system revolves around crafting increasingly powerful weapons from the ores you mine. Without a good weapon, you cannot survive the enemies that guard the deeper caves, and without deeper caves, you cannot access the ores needed for better weapons. The loop is tight and rewarding.

The game currently features over 25 distinct weapon types spanning swords, axes, hammers, bows, daggers, staffs, scythes, and more. Each weapon has a base damage value, an attack speed rating, and a set of required ores that must be gathered before you can forge it. Weapons also come in five quality tiers — Common, Uncommon, Rare, Epic, and Legendary — determined entirely by your performance in the forging minigame. A Legendary version of any weapon deals significantly more damage and has higher durability than its Common counterpart, so mastering the forge is just as important as finding rare ores.

This guide covers every weapon in The Forge organized by type and tier, including full crafting recipes, ore requirements, and a tier list ranking every weapon from S to D. Whether you are a new player looking for your first upgrade or a veteran chasing Mythical-tier gear, this page has everything you need. ## How Forging Works

Forging is the core mechanic that separates The Forge from other Roblox RPGs. Instead of simply combining materials in a menu, you actively participate in a timing-based minigame every time you craft a weapon. The process starts at the anvil — walk up to any anvil in the game world and interact with it. You will see a list of weapon blueprints you have unlocked, each showing the required ores and quantities. Select a blueprint, confirm you have the materials, and the forging minigame begins.

The minigame presents a series of timing markers that move across the screen. Your job is to press the action button when the marker lands inside the target zone. Each weapon blueprint has a set number of strikes — typically between four and eight depending on the weapon type. The closer each strike lands to the center of the target zone, the more quality points you accumulate. After all strikes are complete, your total quality points determine the final weapon quality: Common, Uncommon, Rare, Epic, or Legendary.

Quality has a massive impact on the finished weapon. A Legendary weapon deals roughly 50% more damage than its Common version, has double the durability, and may unlock a special passive effect unique to that weapon. Epic quality sits between those extremes and is a realistic target for most players. Rare quality is the baseline for competitive play — anything below Rare is generally considered a practice run or a weapon you will replace soon.

Different weapon types have different timing patterns in the minigame. Swords use a steady, rhythmic pattern with evenly spaced markers. Hammers have slow, heavy beats with wider target zones but fewer strikes. Bows feature quick bursts of rapid markers that test your reaction speed. Daggers are the fastest, with tight target zones and many strikes in quick succession. Learning the rhythm for each weapon type is essential — a player who masters sword forging may struggle with daggers until they adjust to the different tempo.

If you fail the minigame entirely — missing too many markers or hitting them far from center — the weapon still crafts but at Common quality with reduced stats. You never lose your ores on a failed forge, which is a forgiving design choice that encourages experimentation. Practice on cheaper weapons like the Copper Sword before attempting to forge expensive Mythical-tier gear.

## Weapon Tier List

This tier list ranks every weapon based on overall effectiveness considering damage output, speed, utility, and how easy it is to obtain the required ores. Weapons are evaluated at Legendary quality since that is the target for serious players.

**S-Tier — Best in Game**

- Arcane Staff — Highest raw damage in the game at 160 base. Its Legendary passive fires homing arcane bolts that deal bonus damage. Unmatched for ranged DPS.
- Darkryte Blade — 150 base damage at Fast speed is an absurd combination. Legendary passive grants a shadow dash that phases through enemies. The best melee weapon in The Forge.
- Demonite Scythe — 140 base damage with a lifesteal passive at Legendary quality. Every hit heals you for a percentage of damage dealt. Makes you nearly unkillable in sustained fights.

**A-Tier — Excellent**

- Magma Greatsword — 120 base damage with a fire AoE effect at Epic quality and above. Hits multiple enemies in a wide arc. The best non-Mythical melee weapon.
- Infernal Staff — 110 base damage with a burn-over-time effect. Strong ranged option that does not require Mythical ores.
- Infernal Edge — 135 base damage at Fast speed. A direct upgrade to the Diamond Greatsword and the strongest sword before Mythical tier.
- Diamond Warhammer — 95 base damage with a stun effect on heavy attacks. The stun locks enemies in place for follow-up hits, making it invaluable against bosses.
- Volcanic Greatsword — 130 base damage with lava pool ground effect. Excellent area denial in combat.

**B-Tier — Strong**

- Crystal Bow — 55 base damage at Fast speed with long range. The best ranged weapon before Volcanic Depths content. Pairs well with kiting strategies.
- Frostite Daggers — 40x2 base damage at Very Fast speed. The dual-hit mechanic means effective DPS is higher than the numbers suggest. Frost slow effect at Rare quality and above.
- Frostite Claymore — 105 base damage with frost slow. A solid all-rounder for Frostspire content.
- Obsidian Greataxe — 80 base damage at Very Slow speed. Massive single-hit damage but the speed holds it back against groups.
- Prismarine Katana — 100 base damage at Very Fast speed. Excellent DPS but lacks a strong passive effect.
- Magma Crossbow — 100 base damage ranged weapon. Good alternative to the Crystal Bow for players who prefer slower, harder-hitting ranged attacks.

**C-Tier — Solid Mid-Game**

- Mithril Rapier — 60 base damage at Very Fast speed. Outstanding for its tier and will carry you through the Forgotten Kingdom.
- Diamond Greatsword — 90 base damage. A reliable workhorse weapon for Frostspire content.
- Sapphire Edge — 70 base damage at Fast speed. Good transitional weapon between Crystal and Diamond tier.
- Gold Mace — 45 base damage with a knockback effect. Useful for crowd control in mid-game content.
- Platinum Blade — 42 base damage at Fast speed. Slightly better than the Gold Longsword in every way.
- Obsidian Warhammer — 75 base damage with stun. A budget version of the Diamond Warhammer.

**D-Tier — Starter and Early Game**

- Copper Sword — Your first weapon. Gets the job done in Stonewake's Cross and nothing more.
- Copper Dagger — Low damage but teaches you the dual-wield mechanic early.
- Iron Broadsword — A meaningful upgrade over Copper but quickly outclassed.
- Iron Battleaxe — Slow and low damage. Replaced as soon as you reach the Forgotten Kingdom.
- Silver Blade — The best Stonewake weapon but still D-Tier in the overall picture.

- Tin Shield Bash — More of a defensive tool than a weapon. Niche use only.

- Mithril Spear — Decent reach but the Mithril Rapier is better in almost every situation.
- Crystal Blade — Overshadowed by the Sapphire Edge and Diamond Greatsword.

## Forging Quality Tips

Consistently hitting Legendary quality on the forging minigame is what separates good players from great ones. The difference between a Common and Legendary weapon is enormous — roughly 50% more damage, double durability, and access to passive effects that only activate at Epic quality or above. Here are the strategies that experienced forgers use to hit Legendary consistently.

First, learn the rhythm for each weapon type before using expensive ores. Swords have a steady four-beat pattern with evenly spaced timing markers. The tempo is moderate and predictable, making swords the easiest weapon type to forge at high quality. If you are new to forging, start with swords. Hammers use a slow three-beat pattern with wide target zones — they are forgiving but the slow pace can throw off players who are used to faster rhythms. Bows have quick five-beat bursts that require fast reactions. Daggers are the hardest to forge, with six to eight rapid markers and tight target zones. Staffs use an irregular pattern that alternates between slow and fast beats, requiring you to stay focused throughout.

Second, watch the visual and audio cues. Each timing marker has a subtle glow that intensifies as it approaches the center of the target zone. The anvil also produces a distinct sound at the perfect moment — listen for the sharp ring rather than the dull thud. Many top forgers play with headphones and rely more on audio timing than visual cues.

Third, practice on cheap materials. Forge Copper Swords and Iron Broadswords repeatedly until you can hit Legendary quality on those blueprints at least 80% of the time. The timing patterns scale in difficulty with weapon tier, but the fundamental rhythm stays the same within each weapon type. Mastering the basics on cheap weapons translates directly to better results on expensive ones.

Fourth, reduce input lag. If you are playing on a device with high latency, the timing markers will feel off. Lower your graphics settings if needed, and make sure your connection to the server is stable. Even a small amount of lag can turn a perfect strike into a near-miss.

Finally, some [races](/the-forge-race-tier-list) provide forging bonuses that widen the target zone or slow down the timing markers slightly. If you are serious about forging, consider rerolling for a race with forging-related traits. The difference is subtle but adds up over hundreds of forges.

## Best Weapons for Each Playstyle

Not every player approaches combat the same way. The Forge supports multiple playstyles, and choosing the right weapon for your preferred approach makes a significant difference. Here are the best weapon picks for each major playstyle.

**Combat DPS** — If your goal is to deal the most damage as fast as possible, the Darkryte Blade is the undisputed king. Its 150 base damage at Fast speed gives it the highest sustained DPS of any weapon in the game, and the shadow dash passive lets you reposition instantly during fights. For players who have not yet obtained Darkryte, the Infernal Edge at 135 base damage and Fast speed is the next best option. The Demonite Scythe is also excellent for DPS players who want sustain — its lifesteal passive means you spend less time healing and more time fighting.

**Tank** — Tank players want weapons that control the battlefield. The Diamond Warhammer is the top pick thanks to its stun effect on heavy attacks. Stunning a boss and then following up with a full combo is one of the most effective strategies in the game. The Magma Greatsword is the second choice for tanks — its fire AoE damages everything around you, which is perfect for holding a position against waves of enemies. The Volcanic Greatsword's lava pool effect also works well for area denial, forcing enemies to path around the hazard.

**Ranged** — Ranged players should aim for the Arcane Staff as their end-game weapon. Its 160 base damage and homing arcane bolts make it the strongest ranged option by a wide margin. Before obtaining Arcane Crystals, the Infernal Staff is a strong alternative with its burn-over-time effect. The Crystal Bow is the best mid-game ranged weapon and remains viable well into late-game content thanks to its Fast speed and consistent damage output. The Magma Crossbow bridges the gap between the Crystal Bow and Infernal Staff.

**Speed** — Speed-focused players prioritize attack frequency over raw damage per hit. The Frostite Daggers are the best speed weapon thanks to their dual-hit mechanic and Very Fast attack speed. Each hit applies a frost slow, which means enemies are perpetually slowed while you chip away at their health. The Mithril Rapier is the budget speed option and remains effective well beyond its tier thanks to its Very Fast speed. The Prismarine Katana offers a middle ground — Very Fast speed with higher base damage than the daggers, though it lacks the dual-hit mechanic.

## Weapon and Rune Combinations

Runes add passive effects to your weapons and can dramatically change how a weapon performs in combat. Choosing the right rune for your weapon type is essential for maximizing your build. Here are the best rune pairings for each weapon category.

**Swords** benefit most from the Sharpness Rune, which increases base damage by a flat percentage. Since swords already have balanced stats, amplifying their damage makes them stronger across all situations. The Critical Strike Rune is a close second — it adds a chance for double damage on each hit, which synergizes well with Fast and Very Fast swords like the Darkryte Blade and Mithril Rapier.

**Hammers and Axes** pair best with the Crushing Rune, which increases stun duration and adds a small AoE shockwave on heavy attacks. The Diamond Warhammer with a Crushing Rune has one of the longest stun locks in the game. The Vampiric Rune is also strong on slow weapons — since each hit deals high damage, the percentage-based lifesteal returns a significant amount of health per swing.

**Bows and Staffs** should use the Piercing Rune, which allows projectiles to pass through enemies and hit targets behind them. This turns single-target ranged weapons into pseudo-AoE weapons in tight corridors. The Arcane Staff with a Piercing Rune can clear entire groups of enemies with a single shot. The Elemental Rune is another strong choice, adding bonus elemental damage that scales with the weapon's base damage.

**Daggers** work best with the Venom Rune, which applies a poison effect on each hit. Since daggers hit many times in quick succession, the poison stacks rapidly and deals substantial damage over time. The Haste Rune is the alternative pick — it increases attack speed even further, pushing daggers into absurd attack frequency territory.

## FAQ

### Can you sell weapons to other players or vendors?

Yes. You can sell weapons to NPC vendors in any region for gold. The sell price depends on the weapon type, the ores used, and the quality tier. Legendary weapons sell for significantly more than Common ones. You can also trade weapons directly with other players using the trade system — walk up to another player and initiate a trade. Mythical weapons are the most valuable items in the player trading economy.

### Can weapons break?

Weapons have a durability stat that decreases with use. When durability reaches zero, the weapon deals reduced damage but does not disappear from your inventory. You can repair weapons at any anvil using a small amount of the original ore type. Higher quality weapons have more durability and last longer between repairs. Legendary weapons have roughly double the durability of Common versions, which is another reason to aim for high forging quality.

### What is the best weapon in The Forge?

The Darkryte Blade is generally considered the best overall weapon due to its combination of 150 base damage and Fast attack speed, giving it the highest sustained DPS in the game. However, the Arcane Staff deals more damage per hit at 160 base and is the best ranged weapon. The Demonite Scythe is the best for survivability thanks to its lifesteal passive. The "best" weapon depends on your playstyle — check the playstyle section above for detailed recommendations.

### How do you get Mythical weapons?

Mythical weapons — Demonite Scythe, Darkryte Blade, and Arcane Staff — require 10 of their respective Mythical ores (Demonite, Darkryte, and Arcane Crystal). These ores are extremely rare and spawn in the deepest sections of caves, often behind breakable walls. Farming Mythical ores requires patience, a top-tier [pickaxe](/the-forge-pickaxes-guide), Luck Totems, and ideally a [race](/the-forge-race-tier-list) with luck bonuses. See our [ores guide](/the-forge-ores-guide) for detailed farming strategies and spawn locations.

### Can you trade weapons with other players?

Yes. The Forge supports a player-to-player trading system. Walk up to another player and use the trade interaction to open the trade window. Both players must confirm the trade before it completes. You can trade weapons, ores, and other items. Mythical weapons are the highest-value trade items in the game, so expect to offer substantial resources if you want to acquire one through trading rather than crafting.
